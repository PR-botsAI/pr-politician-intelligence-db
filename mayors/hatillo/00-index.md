## Why Hatillo Mayor Matters

Hatillo is the second municipality in Edgar Robles Rivera's District 14. The Hatillo mayor:
- Shares constituents with Robles (our #1 target)
- Controls the municipal government while Robles controls the legislative representation
- Is a natural "next call" after Robles — political counterpart
- Political relationship between them (same party? rivals?) is strategically important

## Hatillo Profile

### Cultural Identity
- **Festival de Máscaras de Hatillo** (December 28): Annual mask festival, one of PR's most beloved traditions. Celebrants wear elaborate masks and chase children through the streets in a recreation of Herod's soldiers seeking baby Jesus. The festival draws visitors island-wide and from the diaspora. It is Hatillo's most powerful cultural asset and identity marker.
- **Dairy heritage:** Hatillo has been Puerto Rico's dairy capital for generations. The cooperative dairy industry here (Hatillo dairy cooperatives) produces a significant portion of PR's fresh milk. This is both economic reality and cultural identity.

### Economy
- **Dairy industry:** Cooperative-based dairy production; processing plants
- **Retail:** Growing commercial strip on PR-2 corridor
- **Agriculture:** Various farming operations beyond dairy
- **Limited manufacturing:** Some light industry

### Political Profile
- Both PNP and PPD have competed for Hatillo
- Agricultural communities often have PPD leanings (traditional rural base)
- But Hatillo has elected PNP mayors
