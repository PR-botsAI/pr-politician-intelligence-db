# Representative District 19 — Intelligence Profile

## Basic Information
- **Number:** Distrito Representativo 19
- **Current Representative:** Joel Franqui Atiles (PPD, 2025–2029)
- **Municipalities:** Hatillo, Camuy, Quebradillas
- **Region:** Norte (Northwestern coast)
- **Senate District Overlap:** Senatorial Distrito III (Arecibo)

---

## Geographic Overview
District 19 covers three municipalities along the northwestern coast:

**Hatillo** — Dairy capital of Puerto Rico. Festival de Máscaras. Agriculture-heavy. Strong community identity.

**Camuy** — Home to the famous Camuy Caves (Parque de las Cavernas del Río Camuy) — one of the world's largest cave systems; major eco-tourism asset. Agricultural community. Small commercial center.

**Quebradillas** — Coastal municipality known for beaches (Guajataca). Agriculture and fishing. Small town feel. Some tourism. Home of Instit to de Banca y Comercio campus.

## Key Infrastructure

### Water/PRASA
- Same Norte Region PRASA system as District 14
- Camuy and Quebradillas are served by smaller systems that connect to Norte hub
- Service interruptions are a common constituent complaint

### Roads
- PR-2 (coastal highway) — main artery connecting all three municipalities
- Interior roads in worse condition; mountain access limited
- Guajataca area roads (Quebradillas) — some damage from 2017

### Camuy Caves (Tourism Infrastructure)
- The Camuy Caves are a federal (U.S. National Park Service adjacent) / PR government asset
- Damaged and partially closed post-Maria; limited reopening
- Full restoration is a major constituency goal

---

## Key Employers / Economic Anchors

- **Dairy industry (Hatillo):** Suiza Dairy, Vaqueria Tres Monjitas, cooperative processing — hundreds of direct jobs, many more indirect
- **Camuy Caves (tourism):** When fully operational, major economic driver
- **Agriculture:** Coffee, plantains, tropical fruits across all three
- **Government:** Municipal governments are major employers
- **Retail:** Commercial strip on PR-2 corridor

---

## Political Profile

- **Traditional lean:** PPD-leaning — agricultural communities historically PPD
- **Hatillo:** More competitive; dairy community has economic interests that sometimes cross party lines
- **Camuy:** PPD strength in rural sectors; cave tourism community mixed
- **Quebradillas:** Small, tends PPD in municipal races
- **2024:** Franqui won as PPD in what was a PNP wave — shows the district has genuine PPD base
- **2028 outlook:** Lean PPD; Franqui is competitive if he delivers

---

## Community Power Structure

- **Dairy cooperatives:** Organized, influential in Hatillo — any politician needs their support
- **Catholic parishes:** Strong presence in all three municipalities
- **Camuy Caves management:** Whoever helps restore the caves earns major goodwill
- **Municipal mayors:** Working relationship with mayors of all 3 municipalities is essential
- **Facebook groups:** Each municipality has active community Facebook groups (primary info channel)
- **WhatsApp networks:** Barrio and sector WhatsApp groups — news travels fast

## Digital Landscape

- Same pattern as District 14: Facebook-first, WhatsApp-universal, mobile-dominant, Spanish-only
- **Camuy Caves tourism community:** More digital/Instagram-savvy than agricultural base — interesting content opportunity
- **Festival de Máscaras (Hatillo):** December event creates massive social media moment that a good page can capture
