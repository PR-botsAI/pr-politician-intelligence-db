## Key Infrastructure in District

### PRASA (Water)
- Northern Region headquarters located in Arecibo
- Multiple filtration plants serving the region
- Chronic service interruption issues
- **This is the defining infrastructure issue for this district in 2025–2026**

### LUMA Energy
- Standard Puerto Rico electricity issues
- Post-Maria grid still not fully rebuilt in some sectors
- Rural sectors of Hatillo particularly affected

### Roads
- PR-2 (coastal) — primary commercial artery
- PR-22 (express) — connects to San Juan metropolitan area
- PR-10 — connects Arecibo to interior (Lares, Utuado) — critical mountain access road
- Local municipal roads — many still damaged from Hurricane Maria (2017)

## Key Employers / Economic Anchors

### Arecibo
- **Healthcare:** Hospital Dr. Ramón Ruíz Arnau, private clinics, Auxilio Mutuo Arecibo
- **Pharmaceuticals:** Some pharmaceutical manufacturing in the region
- **Retail/Commerce:** Centro Comercial Las Catalinas (regional mall), commercial corridor
- **Government:** Municipal government, federal offices (VA, USDA), Cámara district office
- **Education:** Universidad de Puerto Rico Arecibo, Pontifical Catholic University of PR regional campus

### Hatillo
- **Dairy:** Hatillo is Puerto Rico's dairy capital — major cooperatives and processing
- **Agriculture:** Various agricultural operations
- **Retail:** Growing commercial strip along PR-2
- **Festival tourism:** Festival de Máscaras (December) generates significant economic activity

## Community Organizations (Power Structure)

- **Catholic Church:** Parishes in both Arecibo and Hatillo — significant community anchor
- **UPR Arecibo:** University community influences progressive/intellectual sector
- **Agricultural cooperatives:** Hatillo dairy cooperatives — organized agricultural voice
- **Business associations:** Chamber of commerce in Arecibo
- **Homeowners associations:** Various in residential communities

## Digital Behavior of District 14 Constituents

- **Facebook-first:** Older demographic — Facebook is primary social platform
- **WhatsApp:** Universal — everyone uses WhatsApp for community communication
- **Mobile:** Majority of web browsing is on mobile phones
- **Spanish-language:** Nearly universal — Spanish-first content is essential
- **Local trust:** Constituents respond to content that names their barrio or municipality specifically
- **Key insight:** A lead form that asks "tu barrio" and lists Arecibo and Hatillo sectors by name will have much higher conversion than a generic form
