# Senatorial District V — Ponce

## Overview
- **District Number:** V (Quinto)
- **Region:** Southern Puerto Rico
- **Character:** Historic, industrial, declining economically; proud Ponceño identity

---

## Geographic Coverage
Distrito V covers the Ponce metropolitan area and surrounding southern municipalities:
- **Ponce:** ~138,000; second largest city; "La Ciudad Señorial"
- **Juana Díaz:** ~47,000; agricultural, semi-rural
- **Coamo:** ~40,000; historic town; Coamo hot springs
- **Salinas:** ~28,000; coastal, fishing, saline flats
- **Santa Isabel, Villalba, Corozal** (possibly included)

---

## Character of the South

- **Hotter and drier** than the north (rain shadow effect of the Cordillera Central)
- **Historical industrial base:** Ponce had significant industrial activity in 20th century
- **Population decline:** Ponce has lost significant population over decades
- **The "Massacre de Ponce" (1937):** Police killed 19 independence demonstrators in Ponce — a defining historical moment in PR's independence movement; Ponce has a complex relationship with this history
- **Puerto Rico Museum of Art (Ponce):** World-class art collection; cultural jewel
- **Ponce identity:** Ponceños are notoriously proud of their city; they consider Ponce the "real" PR in contrast to San Juan

---

## 2020 Earthquake Impact
The January 7, 2020 earthquake (6.4 magnitude) and aftershocks hit southern PR hardest:
- Epicenter near Guanica (south)
- Ponce area affected: building damage, power outages
- School closures (many schools were structurally unsafe)
- Economic disruption in already struggling southern region
- Recovery has been slow and controversial

---

## Political Profile

- Ponce is historically divided; PNP founder Luis A. Ferré was from Ponce — gives PNP historical roots here
- PPD also has strong presence
- The south generally is more competitive than the PNP metro strongholds
- Post-earthquake frustration creates political opportunity for challengers

---

## Key Issues

1. **Economic revival:** Reversing Ponce's decline is the central challenge
2. **Post-earthquake recovery:** Infrastructure and housing
3. **Port development:** Ponce's deep-water port as economic asset
4. **Heritage preservation:** Ponce's architecture and history as tourism driver
5. **Healthcare:** South has fewer medical resources per capita than metro
6. **Industrial base:** Attracting new industry to replace what's been lost
