# Senatorial District II — Bayamón

## Overview
- **District Number:** II (Segundo)
- **Current Senator:** Carmelo J. Ríos Santiago (PNP, First Vice President)
- **Region:** Metropolitan Bayamón (northwestern metro area)
- **Character:** Suburban-urban; PR's most populated senatorial district

---

## Municipalities Covered
Distrito II covers Bayamón and surrounding municipalities. Likely includes:
- **Bayamón:** ~170,000 population; most populated municipality in PR
- **Guaynabo:** ~83,000; affluent suburban municipality between San Juan and Bayamón
- **Toa Alta:** ~74,000; suburban/semi-rural
- **Toa Baja:** ~77,000; coastal, industrial

## Demographic Profile

- **Most populated senatorial district** by population (Bayamón alone is largest municipality)
- **Suburban character:** Middle-class families, homeowners, car-dependent
- **Economic diversity:** Guaynabo is affluent; Toa Baja is more working class
- **Pharmaceutical corridor:** Major pharmaceutical manufacturing along PR-22 (AstraZeneca, Johnson & Johnson historically)
- **Shopping:** Largest retail concentration in PR (multiple major malls)

---

## Political Profile

- **Strong PNP territory:** Bayamón-Guaynabo metro is PNP's most reliable stronghold
- Carmelo Ríos' position as First VP of Senate reflects this district's importance to PNP
- PPD has municipal-level presence but legislative races trend PNP
- This is the "safe seat" district for PNP Senate leadership

---

## Key Issues

1. **Traffic:** Bayamón's commercial density + suburban sprawl = PR's worst traffic
2. **Infrastructure:** Water, roads, drainage in rapidly developed areas
3. **Economic development:** Maintaining the pharmaceutical and retail industrial base
4. **Flooding:** Parts of Bayamón flood regularly
5. **Quality of life for suburban families:** Schools, parks, services
6. **LUMA / electricity:** Outages in suburban areas are politically salient for middle-class voters who expect reliability
