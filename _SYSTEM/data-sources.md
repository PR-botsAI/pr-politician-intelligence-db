# Research Data Sources — Where to Find Everything

## Official Government Sources

### Legislative Records
- **SUTRA (Sistema Único de Trámite Legislativo)**
  - URL: sutra.oslpr.org
  - What's there: Every bill, resolution, amendment filed in PR legislature 2001–present
  - Search by: Legislator name, bill number, keyword, session year
  - How to use: Search politician's name → see all bills they've filed or co-sponsored

- **Cámara de Representantes**
  - URL: camara.pr.gov
  - What's there: Representative directory, committee assignments, press releases, session videos
  - For each rep: Bio page, email, phone, district info

- **Senado de Puerto Rico**
  - URL: senado.pr.gov
  - What's there: Senator directory, committee assignments, press releases
  - Full senator list with photos and contact info

- **Directorio Legislativo (SUTRA)**
  - URL: sutra.oslpr.org/directorio
  - What's there: Current term 2025–2028 complete legislator list with party and contact

### Electoral Data
- **Comisión Estatal de Elecciones (CEE)**
  - URL: ceepur.org
  - What's there: Official election results, candidate filings, campaign finance
  - 2024 certified results: November 2024 elections

- **Ballotpedia Puerto Rico**
  - URL: ballotpedia.org/Puerto_Rico
  - What's there: Candidate profiles, election histories, endorsements
  - Best for: Quick electoral biography and vote counts

- **Puerto Rico 2024 Certified Results**
  - URL: mcvpr.com (MCVPR legal alert) — published Dec 30, 2024
  - What's there: Full certified 2024 election results by office

### Municipal / Mayor Data
- **Portal del Estado**
  - URL: estado.pr.gov
  - Each municipality has their own portal (e.g., arecibo.pr.gov, hatillo.pr.gov)
  - Mayor's office contact, municipal legislature info

### Search Strategy for News Research
```
Google search formula:
"[Politician full name]" site:noticel.com
"[Politician full name]" site:elnuevodia.com
"[Politician full name]" (noticel OR elnuevodia OR metro.pr)
```

For date-filtered research:
```
"[Name]" after:2026-01-01 site:noticel.com
```

## Social Media Research

### Facebook
1. Search: "[Full name]" + "Representante" or "Senador" or "Alcalde"
2. Look for verified page (official checkmark, "Político" category)
3. Metrics to collect: Followers, avg. likes/comments last 10 posts, posting frequency, content types

### Instagram
1. Search handle or full name
2. Often linked from their Facebook page

### Twitter/X
1. Many PR politicians are active here
2. Search by name or known handle
3. Good for finding statements and political positions

## Campaign Finance (Limited in PR)
- CEE has some campaign finance disclosure but coverage is inconsistent
- URL: ceepur.org → Finanzas Políticas
- What's available: Contribution reports, expenditure reports for major candidates

---

## Wikipedia (Puerto Rico Political)
- Good for: Historical context, party histories, senior politician bios
- Spanish Wikipedia often has more detail on PR politicians than English
- URL: es.wikipedia.org/wiki/[nombre]
- Cross-reference: Always verify Wikipedia info with primary sources

## Additional Research Tools

### For Bill Research
- SUTRA full text: sutra.oslpr.org → search by legislator’s name → view all medidas
- Filter by: Camara vs Senado, by session year

### For District Geography
- CEE district maps: ceepur.org
- Google Maps for geographic understanding of district
- Census.gov for demographic data (PR is covered by U.S. Census)

### For Demographic Data
- U.S. Census Bureau — Puerto Rico Community Survey
- URL: census.gov/topics/population/race/about.html (PR section)
- Data: Population, income, age, education by municipality

### For Historical Context
- Fundación Luis Muñoz Marín archives (for PPD history)
- Archivo General de Puerto Rico
- University of Puerto Rico political science resources
