# Political Belief System Taxonomy — Puerto Rico

## Overview
This taxonomy is the classification system used to describe and compare the belief systems of Puerto Rico politicians. It covers both PR-specific political dimensions (status question) and universal political dimensions (economic, social, governance).

---

## Dimension 1: Puerto Rico Status Position

This is the defining political question in PR — the one that fundamentally structures party loyalty and political identity more than any other issue.

### The Four Positions

#### 1A. Estadidad (Statehood)
- **Core belief:** Puerto Rico should become the 51st U.S. state
- **Rationale:** Full citizenship rights, congressional representation, end of colonial status, federal parity
- **Party:** PNP (primary)
- **National affiliation:** Republican Party
- **Rhetorical frame:** "Igualdad" (equality), "dignidad" (dignity), "plena ciudadanía"
- **Emotional core:** Frustration with second-class citizenship; desire for formal equality within the U.S.
- **Typical voter:** Urban professional, older voter, business owner, military family
- **Internal variation:** Hard statehooders (must be statehood-only) vs. soft statehooders (accept a process)

#### 1B. Estado Libre Asociado (Commonwealth / Autonomy)
- **Core belief:** Puerto Rico should remain a U.S. territory but with enhanced autonomy
- **Rationale:** Preserves cultural identity, existing federal benefits, avoids forced assimilation
- **Party:** PPD (primary)
- **National affiliation:** Democratic Party
- **Rhetorical frame:** "Soberanía," "identidad puertorriqueña," "la mejor relación"
- **Emotional core:** Pride in Puerto Rican culture and identity; pragmatic fear of independence's economic risks; comfort with current arrangement
- **Typical voter:** Rural voter, older generation, union member, public employee
- **Internal variation:** "Dignified ELA" faction (wants more autonomy) vs. status-quo PPD

#### 1C. Independencia
- **Core belief:** Puerto Rico should be a fully sovereign independent nation
- **Rationale:** Full self-determination, decolonization, cultural sovereignty, end of U.S. imperial relationship
- **Party:** PIP (primary), MVC (allied)
- **National affiliation:** None / progressive internationalist
- **Rhetorical frame:** "Descolonización," "pueblo," "soberanía plena," "autodeterminación"
- **Emotional core:** Anti-colonialism, national pride, justice framework, distrust of U.S. institutions
- **Typical voter:** Intellectual, young progressive, university student, artist/cultural figure
- **Historical note:** Historically suppressed by FBI (COINTELPRO), which gives the movement a resistance identity

#### 1D. Non-Status (Status is Not Priority)
- **Core belief:** Status question is less urgent than governance, economy, daily life issues
- **Party:** Proyecto Dignidad (PD), some MVC
- **Rhetorical frame:** "Lo que importa es el día a día," "los verdaderos problemas son..."
- **Typical voter:** Evangelical Christian (PD), reform-minded voter tired of status debate

## Dimension 2: Economic Ideology

### Left-Right Economic Spectrum in Puerto Rico Context

#### Economic Left
- Strong public sector, pro-union, anti-privatization
- Opposes LUMA (private electricity company), opposes private prison contracts
- Favors rent control, minimum wage increases, worker protections
- Favors tax progressivity
- Parties: PIP, MVC, left wing of PPD

#### Economic Center
- Mixed economy approach
- Accepts some privatization if it improves services
- Favors federal investment and stimulus
- Generally pro-business but with protections
- Parties: Mainstream PPD, centrist PNP

#### Economic Right
- Pro-market, pro-private sector, pro-business
- Favors tax incentives for investors (Act 22/60 defenders)
- Accepts privatization of public utilities
- Favors reducing size of government
- Parties: PNP right wing, Proyecto Dignidad

### Puerto Rico-Specific Economic Issues

**PROMESA / Fiscal Oversight Board (FOMB)**
- Left position: Dissolve the board, cancel the debt, restore sovereignty
- Center position: Work within the board's framework, negotiate
- Right position: Honor the debt obligations, reform through fiscal responsibility

**Tax Incentives (Act 20/22/60 — now Act 60)**
- Pro: Brings investment, jobs, economic development
- Con: Displaces Puerto Ricans, gentrification, tax unfairness

**Federal Funds Dependency**
- All parties acknowledge dependence on Medicaid, nutrition assistance, disaster funds
- Differ on whether this is a feature (maintain) or a problem (develop independence)

## Dimension 3: Social / Cultural Ideology

### Social Conservative
- Opposes same-sex marriage (or is silent on it)
- Pro-life on abortion
- Faith-based values in public policy
- Traditional family structure emphasis
- Parties: Proyecto Dignidad (most explicitly), conservative wing of PNP

### Social Moderate
- Accepts established rights but doesn't campaign on social issues
- Typical of most PNP and PPD mainstream politicians
- "Don't ask, don't tell" approach to LGBT issues

### Social Progressive
- Explicitly pro-LGBTQ+ rights
- Pro-choice or openly discusses reproductive rights
- Anti-racism, feminist framework
- Parties: MVC, PIP, left PPD

## Dimension 4: Governance Style

### Institutionalist
- Operates within party machinery and government structures
- Favors legislation over direct action
- Values committee process, coalition building
- Typical of: Long-serving senators, party leadership

### Populist
- Appeals directly to the people over institutions
- Uses social media to build direct relationship with constituents
- May challenge party leadership
- Typical of: MVC politicians, some new PNP reps

### Technocratic
- Emphasizes data, expertise, management competence
- Less ideological, more problem-solving focused
- Typical of: Politicians with business/professional backgrounds

### Activist-Legislator
- Sees elected office as platform for social movements
- Blurs line between legislator and community organizer
- Typical of: Mariana Nogales, José Vargas Vidot

---

## Dimension 5: Relationship with Power

### Party Loyalist
- Votes with party almost always
- Advances within party structure
- Doesn't rock the boat

### Maverick
- Willing to break with party on key votes
- Builds personal brand separate from party
- More interesting to constituents, more difficult for party leadership

### Machine Politician
- Embedded in patronage networks
- Delivers services and jobs to constituents in exchange for votes
- Old-school PR political model

### Reform Politician
- Campaigns against corruption, machine politics
- Emphasizes transparency, ethics reform
- Often newer politician or from smaller party
