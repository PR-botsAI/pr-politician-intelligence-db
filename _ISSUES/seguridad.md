# Issue Intelligence: Seguridad (Public Safety & Crime)

## Why This Issue Matters
Crime and public safety consistently rank as top concerns in Puerto Rico polls. High murder rates, drug trafficking, and police reform debates make this a powerful political issue — one where every politician must have a position.

---

## Puerto Rico Crime Reality

### Murder Rate
- Puerto Rico historically has one of the highest murder rates in the U.S. jurisdictions
- Rate has varied: peaked around 2011 (1,136 murders), declined with some gang suppression efforts, remains elevated
- Concentrated in specific high-risk municipalities: Bayamón, Guaynabo, parts of San Juan, Ponce
- Norte region: Arecibo has some crime but not at metro levels
- **Context:** Murder rates are concentrated in gang/drug trafficking zones; tourist areas and suburban municipalities are much safer

### Drug Trafficking
- PR is a major transit point for drugs moving from South America to U.S. mainland
- Caribbean location and port infrastructure make it strategically important for traffickers
- Local drug markets fuel violence in housing projects and urban areas
- Federal agencies (DEA, FBI) operate extensively in PR

### Police Context
- **Puerto Rico Police Bureau (PRPB):** Under federal consent decree since 2013 due to documented civil rights violations
- Reform is ongoing — federal oversight was partially lifted but issues remain
- Understaffing is chronic — PRPB has fewer officers than needed
- Low salaries cause turnover — experienced officers leave for mainland or private security

## Party Positions on Security

### PNP
- Law-and-order emphasis
- More police, better equipment, stronger enforcement
- Supports federal collaboration (DEA, FBI, National Guard)
- Historically favored mandatory minimum sentences
- Death penalty debate: PR abolished it but some PNP conservatives support it

### PPD
- Law-and-order but with reform emphasis
- Address root causes (poverty, drug addiction) as part of crime solution
- More community policing emphasis
- Critiques police brutality as counterproductive

### MVC/PIP
- Police reform as civil rights issue — federal consent decree important
- Address root causes: poverty, addiction, inequality drive crime
- Oppose mass incarceration approach
- Community-based violence prevention
- Skeptical of military-style policing

### Proyecto Dignidad
- Strong law-and-order
- Faith-based rehabilitation and prevention
- Family stability as crime prevention
- Anti-drug from moral/faith perspective

---

## Security Issues by District Type

### Urban (San Juan, Bayamón, Ponce)
- Violent crime, gang activity
- Drug market competition
- Police presence and response time

### Norte Region (Arecibo, Hatillo, Camuy)
- Lower violent crime than metro areas
- Drug trafficking routes (PR-22 corridor)
- Property crime
- Response time in rural sectors
- Domestic violence (a significant issue in rural PR)

### Mountain Interior
- Isolation means slow police response
- Domestic violence hidden in isolated communities
- Drug trafficking through mountain routes

## How to Use in Landing Pages

- **For any politician:** Frame security as "protecting our families" — universal language
- **Norte region:** Lead with community safety, not urban crime stats — different problem profile
- **Issue card:** "🚫 Seguridad para Nuestras Familias — [Name] trabaja para que nuestras calles sean seguras"
- Avoid specific crime statistics unless positive (crime down under their tenure)
- Connect to community programs, not just enforcement
