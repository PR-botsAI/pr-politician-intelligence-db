# Issue Intelligence: Agua y Infraestructura (Water & Infrastructure)

## Why This Issue Matters for Our Campaign
Water and infrastructure is the #1 constituent complaint across Puerto Rico, particularly in the Norte region. It is Edgar Robles Rivera's signature issue, affects nearly every politician in the island, and is a powerful hook for demonstrating how a digital platform can help legislators respond to constituent concerns.

---

## The PRASA / AAA Crisis

### What is PRASA/AAA?
- **PRASA** = Puerto Rico Aqueduct and Sewer Authority (Autoridad de Acueductos y Alcantarillados — AAA)
- **Type:** Public corporation of the Government of Puerto Rico
- **Function:** Manages water supply and wastewater treatment for 96% of Puerto Rico's population
- **Scale:** Serves ~3.4 million people; operates 114 water systems; 52 wastewater systems
- **Federal oversight:** Subject to EPA regulations; has ongoing EPA compliance orders

### PRASA's Chronic Problems

#### Infrastructure Age
- PR's water infrastructure is decades old
- Many pipes are 50–80 years old
- Hurricane Maria (2017) damaged ~50% of water infrastructure; much still not fully repaired
- Earthquakes (2020) caused additional damage in southwest

#### Service Interruptions
- Frequent water cuts (“tandeos”) across the island
- In the Norte region, Arecibo and Hatillo experience regular multi-hour to multi-day outages
- New connection delays (new homes/businesses waiting months for water connection)
- Leaks: Significant water loss from pipe leaks that PRASA is slow to repair

#### Administrative Instability
- High executive turnover — multiple executive directors in recent years
- Regional directors frequently changed
- **The Robles case (March 2026):** 3 directors of Northern Region in 8 days is an extreme example of systemic instability

#### Operational Complexity
- Filtration plants require specialized technical operation
- High turnover in technical staff
- Remote mountain communities are difficult to serve
- Multiple aging filtration plants in various states of repair

### Norte Region Specifics
- Northern Region covers: Arecibo, Hatillo, Camuy, Quebradillas, Lares, Utuado, Manatí area
- Key filtration plants: Arecibo is the hub for regional water treatment
- The region depends on the Dos Bocas reservoir system and mountain river sources
- Climate variability (droughts) stresses the system seasonally

## LUMA Energy Crisis

### What is LUMA?
- **LUMA Energy:** Private company contracted to operate Puerto Rico's electricity transmission and distribution
- **Contract:** 15-year contract with Puerto Rico (signed 2021)
- **Operator since:** June 2021
- **Background:** Before LUMA, PREPA (Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority) was the public utility; PREPA went bankrupt; LUMA brought in to manage the system

### Why It's Controversial
- **Rate increases:** Electricity bills skyrocketed after LUMA took over
- **Service quality:** Outages remained frequent despite promises of improvement
- **Post-Maria:** Grid was already damaged; LUMA inherited a broken system
- **Accountability:** Private company is harder for legislators to control than a public entity
- **Anti-privatization sentiment:** Strong public opposition to having a private company run essential utilities

### Political Positions by Party
| Party | Position on LUMA |
|-------|------------------|
| PNP | Generally supportive (they approved the contract); pragmatically accepting |
| PPD | Critical; some call for contract cancellation |
| MVC | Strongly anti-LUMA; calls for public ownership |
| PIP | Strongly anti-LUMA; frames as colonial privatization |

## Infrastructure Broader Context

### Roads
- Hurricane Maria destroyed or damaged thousands of km of roads
- Federal FHWA funding has flowed in but implementation is slow
- Mountain municipalities (Lares, Utuado, Jayuya) have worst road damage
- Interior roads (PR-10 from Arecibo to interior) are critical lifelines
- Municipal roads (not state roads) have less funding and are worse condition

### Broadband
- Puerto Rico has significant broadband desert areas, especially rural/mountain
- Federal funding (FCC, USDA) is flowing for expansion
- Mobile connectivity is better than fixed broadband in many areas
- **Implication for us:** Many of our target constituents primarily use mobile internet
