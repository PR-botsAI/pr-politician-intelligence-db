# Issue Intelligence: Energía (Energy / Electricity)

## Why This Issue Matters
The electricity crisis is the most universally unpopular issue in Puerto Rico across all political lines. LUMA Energy became a lightning rod for anger post-Maria. Every politician has to address it; the positions vary dramatically by party.

---

## The Puerto Rico Electricity Crisis

### Pre-LUMA: PREPA
- **PREPA** (Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority) was the public electric utility for decades
- PREPA went bankrupt — accumulated billions in debt from mismanagement, corruption, deferred maintenance
- Hurricane Maria (2017) destroyed ~80% of the grid — the largest blackout in U.S. history
- Maria restoration took 11 months for some areas — the slowest in U.S. history
- PREPA's debt became part of PR's bankruptcy process

### LUMA Energy (2021–present)
- A private consortium (**LUMA Energy**) was contracted to operate PR's electricity transmission and distribution system in June 2021
- 15-year contract
- **The promise:** LUMA would modernize the grid, improve reliability, and bring private-sector efficiency
- **The reality:** Rates increased dramatically; outages continued; public anger exploded

### LUMA Controversy Details

**Rate Increases:**
- Electricity bills increased significantly after LUMA took over
- Some customers saw bills double or triple
- Residents on fixed incomes (elderly) hit especially hard
- Business owners unable to plan due to cost volatility

**Service Quality:**
- Outages remained frequent, especially in rural areas
- LUMA inherited a broken grid and argues it needs time to rebuild
- Public perception: things didn't improve despite private sector takeover
- Response times to outages: Contentious — LUMA reports one number; municipalities report another

**Accountability Problem:**
- Private company is harder for legislators to control than public utility
- LUMA contracts have provisions limiting PR government's ability to cancel
- Transparency: Less public accountability than PREPA had

**Generation Separation:**
- PREPA still owns the generation plants (power plants)
- A separate company (New Fortress Energy) contracted for gas generation
- LUMA only does transmission/distribution — so the grid has multiple private operators
- This creates accountability gaps

## Renewable Energy Mandate
- PR has a **100% renewable energy by 2050** mandate (Act 17 of 2019)
- Interim goals: 40% by 2025, 60% by 2040
- **Reality:** PR is far behind these targets
- Rooftop solar: PR has one of the highest residential solar adoption rates in the U.S. — partly because people don't trust LUMA
- Battery storage: Growing; Puerto Ricans investing in personal energy independence

## Norte Region Energy Specifics

- Rural sectors of Hatillo, Camuy, Quebradillas experience some of the longest outage durations
- Mountain interior (Lares, Utuado): Outages can last days in extreme weather
- Arecibo urban core: Better service than rural, still frequent short outages
- **Rooftop solar adoption:** High in Norte region; many families and businesses are partially or fully solar
