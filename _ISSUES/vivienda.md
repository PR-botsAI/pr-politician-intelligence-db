# Issue Intelligence: Vivienda (Housing)

## Why This Issue Matters
Housing is a crisis intersecting with gentrification, Act 60 investor displacement, post-hurricane recovery, and homelessness. It is Mariana Nogales and MVC's most powerful economic issue; it affects all districts.

---

## Puerto Rico Housing Landscape

### The Affordability Crisis
- Pre-Maria: PR had relatively affordable housing by U.S. standards
- Post-Maria + Act 60: Significant price increases, especially in desirable areas
- San Juan (Santurce, Old San Juan, Condado): Prices rivaling mainland cities
- Río Grande, Dorado, Rincón: Luxury development pricing out locals

### Act 60 and Gentrification
- **The mechanism:** Act 60 investors move to PR with 0% capital gains tax; they can afford to pay much more for housing than locals
- **The effect:** Real estate prices rise; Puerto Rican renters and buyers are priced out
- **Documented cases:** Santurce has seen mass displacement; some barrios transformed completely
- **Political response:** MVC/PIP have made this their core housing issue; Nogales has documented specific cases
- **PNP response:** Dispute the displacement claims; argue investment benefits overall

### Post-Hurricane Housing
- Hurricane Maria destroyed or severely damaged ~300,000 homes
- FEMA and HUD recovery funds: Billions allocated but implementation has been notoriously slow
- CDBG-DR (Community Development Block Grant Disaster Recovery) funds: $20+ billion allocated; still flowing into 2026
- Many families still in damaged homes; some blue-tarp situations still exist in remote areas 7+ years post-Maria

### Homelessness
- Visible homeless population in San Juan (Santurce, Santurce waterfront, Condado)
- Arecibo area: Some urban homelessness visible
- Root causes: Drug addiction, mental illness, domestic violence displacement, economic crisis
- José Vargas Vidot's work is the most prominent political response to homelessness

### Public Housing (Residenciales)
- PR has large public housing projects (“residenciales”) built in mid-20th century
- Many are in poor condition
- Security issues — gang activity in some residenciales
- Federal HUD oversight
- Resident populations are a significant constituency for legislators representing urban areas

## Norte Region Housing Specifics

- **Less gentrification pressure** than San Juan metro — Arecibo/Hatillo are not Act 60 hotspots
- **Hurricane recovery:** Some Norte rural sectors still have incomplete recovery
- **Aging housing stock:** Many homes are old; repairs are financially out of reach for poor residents
- **Rural isolation:** Remote barrios have housing issues with no political visibility
- **CDBG-DR opportunity:** The legislator who helps constituents navigate FEMA/recovery funds becomes very popular
