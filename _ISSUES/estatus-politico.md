# Issue Intelligence: Estatus Político (Political Status)

## Why This Issue Matters
The political status question — statehood, ELA, or independence — is Puerto Rico's defining political question. It structures party loyalty more than any other issue and appears in every election. Understanding it is essential to understanding every politician in this database.

---

## The Three Options

### Option 1: Estadidad (Statehood)
**What it means:**
- Puerto Rico becomes the 51st state of the United States
- Puerto Ricans gain voting representation in U.S. Congress (2 senators, ~4-5 representatives)
- Puerto Ricans gain presidential vote
- Federal laws apply equally (Medicaid parity, SSI, full federal benefits)
- Spanish can remain official language alongside English

**Who supports it:** PNP (primary), large plurality of Puerto Ricans based on plebiscites

**The process:** Requires act of U.S. Congress; Congress must vote to admit PR as state

**Obstacles:**
- Republican concern: 2 new senators would likely be Democrat
- Democratic concern: Mixed; some support, some hesitant
- The PR Puerto Rican Democracy Act has passed House but not Senate

### Option 2: Estado Libre Asociado / Enhanced Commonwealth
**What it means:**
- Maintain current territorial relationship with U.S.
- OR: Negotiate an "enhanced" ELA with more autonomy
- Puerto Ricans remain U.S. citizens
- No voting representation in Congress
- Federal laws apply as Congress decides (unequal)

**Who supports it:** PPD (primary), but support is declining

**The problem:** Multiple plebiscites have shown declining support for ELA; and the U.S. Supreme Court has ruled the current ELA is just a territory — not a compact between equals

### Option 3: Independencia (Independence)
**What it means:**
- Puerto Rico becomes a fully sovereign independent nation
- Controls its own trade, foreign policy, military, currency
- Full self-determination

**Who supports it:** PIP (primary), many MVC members

**The problem:** Economic transition would be difficult; federal benefits would end

**Counterargument (PIP):** Colonial dependency has not solved PR's economic crisis either; independence allows PR to design its own economic model

## The Plebiscite History

| Year | Result | Note |
|------|--------|------|
| 1967 | ELA 60%, Statehood 39% | First status plebiscite |
| 1993 | ELA 49%, Statehood 46% | Close; ELA barely wins |
| 1998 | "None of the above" 50% | PNP dispute over ballot options |
| 2012 | Statehood 61% (of yes vote) | First time statehood wins plurality |
| 2017 | Statehood 97% (PPD boycotted) | Low turnout; boycott |
| 2020 | Statehood 52.5% | First majority for statehood in valid vote |

**Trend:** Growing statehood support over time; ELA support declining.

---

## Why Congress Hasn't Acted

1. **Partisan calculation:** PR statehood likely adds 2 Democratic senators (PR trends Democratic federally)
2. **Fiscal cost:** Full Medicaid/SSI parity for PR would cost federal government ~$3-5 billion/year
3. **Distraction:** Congress has many other priorities
4. **Legal complexity:** Compact need to be defined, legal questions resolved
5. **PR's own division:** Even with statehood winning plebiscites, a divided PR makes Congress reluctant

## How Status Appears in Landing Pages

### For PNP Politicians
- Use statehood language in badge: "🇵🇺 Luchando por la Estadidad"
- Frame federal benefits as "what statehood would guarantee permanently"
- Mention statehood advocacy as an achievement
- Don't lead with status — lead with constituent services, then connect

### For PPD Politicians
- Avoid leading with ELA — it's a weakening brand
- Focus on governance, services, community — status is secondary
- If mentioned: "Nuestra identidad puertorriqueña primero"

### For MVC/PIP Politicians
- Decolonization frame: "Somos un pueblo que merece decidir su propio futuro"
- Don't necessarily say "independence" in constituent-facing materials — use self-determination language
- Connect status to economic justice issues

### For All Politicians
- Constituent-facing landing pages should NOT lead with status — lead with services
- Status appears as a belief/value in the belief section, not as the primary pitch
- Exception: If they're known primarily as a status crusader (Rivera Schatz)
