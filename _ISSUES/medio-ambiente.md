# Issue Intelligence: Medio Ambiente (Environment)

## Why This Issue Matters
Environmental issues in Puerto Rico are inseparable from public health, climate vulnerability, and colonial exploitation. PR faces sea level rise, more intense hurricanes, petrochemical pollution, and water contamination issues that affect constituents directly.

---

## Puerto Rico Environmental Issues

### Climate Vulnerability
- PR sits in the Caribbean hurricane belt — direct hit risk every season
- Maria (2017) was Category 4 at landfall — caused $90+ billion in damage
- Climate change is making storms more intense and rainfall events more extreme
- Flooding: Urban flooding in San Juan, Ponce; river flooding in mountain communities
- Drought: PR has experienced significant droughts affecting water supply
- **Political consequence:** Environmental resilience is now a mainstream political issue, not a niche one

### Water Contamination
- Agricultural runoff affects some water systems
- Industrial contamination: Some areas near former industrial sites have contaminated groundwater
- PRASA quality: Most PRASA water meets federal standards but violations occur
- Rural wells: Some rural communities on private wells have quality issues

### Petrochemical Industry (South Coast)
- Guayanilla and Peñuelas municipalities host major petrochemical facilities
- AES coal plant in Guayama: Burns coal; significant air quality and ash disposal controversy
- South coast communities have organized environmental justice movements
- MVC/PIP strongly oppose petrochemical expansion
- PNP: More pragmatic — economic jobs vs. environmental concern balance

### Renewable Energy Transition
- See _ISSUES/energia.md for full LUMA/energy context
- Renewable energy is simultaneously an environmental and economic issue
- Solar adoption is driven by both environmental values and practical frustration with LUMA

### Solid Waste
- Landfill capacity crisis: PR's landfills are approaching capacity or already beyond
- Recycling infrastructure is inadequate
- Illegal dumping is common in rural areas
- Post-hurricane debris disposal was a massive challenge

### Protected Natural Areas
- El Yunque National Forest: Only tropical rainforest in U.S. National Forest system; damaged by Maria but recovering
- Bioluminescent bays: Mosquito Bay (Vieques), La Parguera, Laguna Grande — major tourism/ecological assets
- Coral reefs: Degraded but present; fishing and dive tourism depend on them
- Camuy Caves: Ecological treasure + tourism asset

## Norte Region Environmental Specifics

- **Arecibo:**
  - Dos Bocas reservoir and northern river systems — critical water source for region
  - Coastal flooding risk in low-lying sectors
  - Former Arecibo Observatory site — cleanup and repurposing now Arecibo Institute
- **Hatillo:**
  - Dairy farming creates agricultural runoff concerns
  - Coastal area
- **Camuy:**
  - Camuy River and cave system — ecologically unique; protection is a constituency priority
  - The caves are one of PR's most significant ecological and tourist assets
