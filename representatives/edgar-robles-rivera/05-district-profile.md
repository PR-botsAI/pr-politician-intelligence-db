# District Profile — Distrito 14 (Arecibo & Hatillo)

## Geographic Overview
- **District number:** 14
- **Municipalities covered:** Arecibo (parts) + Hatillo
- **Region:** Norte (Northern Puerto Rico)
- **Coastline:** Both municipalities are on the northern Atlantic coast
- **Distance from San Juan:** Arecibo ~75 km west; Hatillo ~90 km west

---

## Arecibo Municipality

### Basic Facts
- **Population:** ~87,000 (declining; was ~100K+ pre-Maria)
- **Area:** 326 km² (one of PR's largest municipalities)
- **Municipal seat:** Arecibo
- **Known for:** Arecibo Observatory (collapsed 2020, now Arecibo Institute), fishing port, agriculture, regional commerce hub
- **Current mayor:** 2024 election results — research current mayor

### Economy
- **Industries:** Retail, healthcare, pharmaceuticals (regional facilities), agriculture, fishing
- **Regional hub:** Arecibo serves as the commercial, medical, and governmental center for the entire Norte region (Hatillo, Camuy, Quebradillas, Lares, Utuado)
- **Federal presence:** Veterans hospital, USDA offices, federal courts
- **Economic challenge:** Manufacturing decline, brain drain to metro area and U.S. mainland

### Key Infrastructure
- **PRASA Northern Region headquarters:** Located in Arecibo — this is why the PRASA issue is so significant for Robles
- **Hospital:** Hospital Dr. Ramón Ruíz Arnau and other facilities — regional medical center
- **Roads:** PR-22 (Luis A. Ferré Expressway), PR-10 (interior access), coastal PR-2
- **Port:** Small fishing port

### Demographics
- Older population (youth emigration is high)
- Mixed socioeconomic: middle class in casco urbano, working class/rural in outlying sectors
- Strong Catholic tradition
- Veterans community (Naval base history)

### Key Barrios / Sectors
- Casco Urbano (downtown)
- Hato Abajo, Hato Arriba
- Miraflores, Palmas Altas
- Dominguito, Garrochales
- Factor, Islote
- Multiple rural barrios surrounding the city

## Hatillo Municipality

### Basic Facts
- **Population:** ~40,000
- **Area:** 104 km²
- **Known for:** Festival de Máscaras (December — famous mask festival), dairy farming, gastronomy
- **Nickname:** "La Ciudad de las Máscaras"
- **Current mayor:** 2024 election results — research current mayor

### Economy
- **Primary industry:** Dairy farming — Hatillo is Puerto Rico's dairy capital
  - Home to major dairy cooperatives and processing plants
  - Leche pasteurizada de Hatillo is a PR cultural staple
- **Retail:** Growing commercial corridor on PR-2
- **Tourism:** Festival de Máscaras draws significant annual attention

### Demographics
- More rural/suburban than Arecibo
- Strong agricultural identity
- Tight-knit community culture
- Strong Catholic tradition

## Top 5 Constituent Issues in Distrito 14

### 1. Agua y PRASA (💧 Top Issue)
The Northern Region of PRASA has chronic problems:
- Service interruptions (cuts in water service lasting hours to days)
- Aging infrastructure with frequent pipe breaks
- Delayed new connections for homes and businesses
- Administrative instability (as Robles publicly documented in March 2026)
- **Why it matters here specifically:** The Norte region is served by filtration plants that require expert operation; the mountains funnel precipitation that PRASA must manage; Arecibo is the regional hub for this infrastructure

### 2. Economía y Empleo
- Limited high-wage employment compared to metro area
- Young people leave for San Juan or U.S. mainland
- Agricultural sector (dairy in Hatillo) needs support
- Need for economic diversification

### 3. Infraestructura Vial (Roads)
- Interior roads between Arecibo and mountain municipalities damaged by Hurricane Maria (2017)
- Many roads still not fully repaired
- PR-10 access to interior is critical for Utuado, Lares connectivity

### 4. Servicios de Salud
- Specialist care access: many residents travel to San Juan for specialists
- Hospital capacity and quality
- Mental health services gap

### 5. Seguridad
- Urban crime in Arecibo casco
- Drug trafficking routes through the Norte corridor
- Police resource distribution (Norte region vs. metro)

## Political Profile of District

- **Traditional lean:** Competitive — historically alternated PNP/PPD
- **2024:** Robles won with PNP as part of island-wide PNP wave
- **Vulnerability:** In a normal (non-wave) election, this district is competitive
- **PPD strength:** Hatillo's agricultural community has some PPD tradition
- **PNP strength:** Arecibo's urban and business community
- **2028 outlook:** Competitive — Robles will need to demonstrate tangible results
