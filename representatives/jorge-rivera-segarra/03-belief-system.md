## The Lares Challenge
Lares is famous for the Grito de Lares (September 23, 1868) — the most significant independence revolt in Puerto Rican history. Every September 23, independence supporters march to Lares. For a PNP representative to serve Lares means navigating deep cultural identity that doesn't align with statehood.

His approach is likely to: emphasize local service, avoid provoking status debates in Lares, focus on recovery and development that transcends status.

---

## Key Issues He Must Navigate

### Hurricane Maria Recovery (2017 — ongoing)
The mountain municipalities suffered the most from Maria:
- Utuado: Massive infrastructure damage; families without power for 11+ months
- Adjuntas: Road access cut off; community mutual aid (Casa Pueblo's solar microgrids became famous)
- Jayuya: Remote communities hardest hit
This is his defining constituent issue — delivering federal recovery funds to his communities.

### 2020 Earthquakes
The January 2020 earthquake sequence (largest 6.4 magnitude) hit southwestern PR hardest but sent tremors through the mountain region. Some communities in his district felt effects.

### Coffee Agriculture
Lares, Utuado, and Adjuntas are historic coffee country. Puerto Rican coffee (once world-famous; supplied the Vatican) was devastated by Maria. Revival is both economic development and cultural identity.
