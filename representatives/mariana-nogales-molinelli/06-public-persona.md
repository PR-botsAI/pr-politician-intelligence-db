# Public Persona & Media Presence — Mariana Nogales Molinelli

## Brand Summary

Mariana Nogales is Puerto Rico's most digitally sophisticated legislator — a lawyer who broke the mold of what a Puerto Rican politician looks and sounds like. She is transparent where others are opaque, specific where others are vague, and willing to hold everyone accountable including her own coalition. Her brand is radical honesty delivered with legal precision.

---

## Communication Style

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tone | Direct, clear, urgent but controlled |
| Language | Spanish-dominant; bilingual when engaging U.S. audiences |
| Register | Legal-precise but accessible — explains complexity without dumbing down |
| Emotional register | Passionate conviction, not performative anger |
| Post length | Long-form by PR standards — her followers actually read it |
| Hashtags | Strategic — creates campaigns, not just tags |
| Response to critics | Direct and substantive — engages the argument, not the insult |

## Instagram (Primary Platform)

- **Handle:** Research to confirm current handle
- **Following:** Among the largest of any PR legislator — estimated 100,000+ (research exact number)
- **Content type:** Legislation breakdowns (her signature), floor speech clips, community events, personal moments that humanize her
- **Frequency:** Multiple times per week; higher during legislative sessions
- **Visual style:** Clean, text-heavy infographics for issue explanations; personal photos to maintain relatability
- **Stories:** Uses stories for real-time legislative commentary during sessions
- **What performs best:** "Leamos el proyecto" (Let's read the bill) posts — people share them widely

## Twitter/X

- Active real-time commentary on political news
- Engages with journalists, political commentators, other politicians
- More rapid-fire than Instagram — quick takes and threads
- Her legal precision shows in Twitter threads: numbered points, citations, precise language
- Connects with Puerto Rican diaspora who follow PR politics from the mainland

## Facebook

- Less dominant for her than Instagram/Twitter
- Still active for community event announcements and older audience reach
- Her content translates across platforms but Instagram is where she built her brand

## Media Coverage

**Primary outlets that cover her:**
- NotiCel (most consistent coverage of her legislative work)
- El Nuevo Día
- Metro Puerto Rico
- Caribbean Business (when relevant to economic policy)
- U.S. media: She has been covered by national U.S. media (NYT, Washington Post) in pieces about Puerto Rico's political transformation — unusual for a minority party state legislator

**Relationship with press:**
- Proactive: She generates news through her social media that press then covers
- Accessible: Generally available for comment and interview
- Not afraid of controversy: Her willingness to name names makes her a reliable news source

---

## Signature Phrases & Voice

- **"Leamos el proyecto"** — Let's read the bill (her most iconic phrase)
- Language of transparency: "El pueblo tiene derecho a saber" (The people have the right to know)
- Accountability framing: "¿Quién se beneficia?" (Who benefits?) — always asking whose interests legislation serves
- Justice framing: "Esto es una injusticia" stated plainly, not dramatically
- Legal precision: Cites specific article numbers, section references — not vague

---

## How She Is Different from Every Other PR Politician

1. She reads bills in real time and shares what she finds
2. She explains the process — how the legislature works, why things happen — not just outcomes
3. She holds coalition partners accountable (rare in PR politics where party loyalty is absolute)
4. She engages with her critics substantively rather than dismissing them
5. She talks about herself less than other politicians — her content is about issues and accountability, not personal brand
6. She has built an audience who trust her because she has earned it with consistent accuracy
