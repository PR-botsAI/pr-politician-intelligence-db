# District Profile — Mariana Nogales Molinelli (Por Acumulación)

## What "Por Acumulación" Means

A representative por acumulación does not represent a specific geographic district — they are elected island-wide and represent all Puerto Rico. There are 11 at-large seats in the Cámara de Representantes (split among parties based on total vote shares).

This means:
- No single barrio or municipality is "hers" exclusively
- She must serve constituents from Arecibo to Humacao, from Ponce to Carolina
- Her constituent base is self-selecting: people who voted for her or MVC, wherever they live
- She cannot focus solely on local infrastructure (no single district's roads are "her roads")
- She compensates by focusing on **island-wide issues** that affect everyone

---

## Her Actual Constituency: Where Her Votes Come From

While formal at-large, Nogales' vote base is geographically concentrated:

**Primary base:**
- **San Juan / Santurce:** Her strongest base. Santurce is the arts/culture/LGBTQ+ hub of PR — densely populated with her core voter profile
- **Río Piedras:** UPR main campus area — students, faculty, young professionals
- **Condado/Miramar:** Progressive professionals, LGBTQ+ community
- **Hato Rey:** Young professionals who work in the financial district but live progressive values

**Secondary base:**
- University towns: UPR Arecibo, UPR Mayagüez, UPR Ponce campus areas
- Other urban progressive pockets across the island
- Digital constituency: people who follow her on Instagram/Twitter from anywhere in PR — she serves them through information, advocacy, and accessibility

---

## How an At-Large Rep Serves Constituents

Without a geographic district, Nogales serves constituents through:

1. **Legislative advocacy on systemic issues** — LUMA, PROMESA, housing, LGBTQ+ rights affect everyone
2. **Direct constituent communication** — DMs, comments, email (any Puerto Rican can contact her)
3. **Information platform** — her explanations of legislation serve hundreds of thousands of followers
4. **Committee work** — even as minority, she can interrogate officials on behalf of all constituents
5. **Case work** — her office helps individual constituents with government problems (same as district reps)
