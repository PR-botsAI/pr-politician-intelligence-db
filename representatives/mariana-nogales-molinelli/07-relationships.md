# Political Relationships — Mariana Nogales Molinelli

## Within MVC

**Manuel Natal Albelo (MVC Coordinator General):**
- The organizational relationship: Natal is the party builder; Nogales is the public face
- Different but complementary roles: Natal handles internal party mechanics; Nogales handles public communication and legislative visibility
- They work in the same coalition; publicly aligned
- Tension potential: As the more famous figure, Nogales' brand can overshadow the party's institutional identity

**Alexandra Lúgaro:**
- Lúgaro ran for governor under MVC banner in 2020 (~14%)
- Both represent the progressive independent spirit of MVC
- Lúgaro is not a current legislator; her relationship with the party vs. as independent figure is complex
- Both are seen as the face of progressive politics in PR

## Alianza de País (MVC + PIP)

**Juan Dalmau Ramírez (PIP President):**
- The Alianza formalized the MVC-PIP alliance for 2024
- Dalmau ran for governor as the Alianza's candidate
- Nogales and Dalmau share ideological alignment: decolonization, progressive economics, anti-corruption
- Different styles: Dalmau is more traditionally charismatic/populist; Nogales more technocratic-transparent
- The alliance is their shared political project

**José Vargas Vidot (MVC Senator):**
- Closest Senate ally; both MVC-aligned
- Different backgrounds (he's a physician/social worker; she's a lawyer) but compatible values
- Both do constituent work that goes beyond typical politician behavior

## Legislative Allies (Across Parties)

- Some PPD colleagues will align on specific issues (anti-LUMA, anti-austerity) even across party lines
- She is willing to praise PNP members when they take good positions — this is unusual and earns credibility
- Her principle: the issue, not the party, determines her position

## Adversaries

**PNP Leadership (Rivera Schatz, etc.):**
- Ideological opposites on virtually every issue
- Rivera Schatz has clashed with progressive minority legislators
- She documents and publicizes the PNP majority's legislative maneuvers — making her an institutional threat to their message control

**Status Quo Politicians (both parties):**
- She explicitly criticizes the "bipartidismo" (two-party system) as corrupt
- PPD members who are seen as too cozy with the system are also targets of her accountability work

## Civil Society Network

- LGBTQ+ organizations in Puerto Rico: allies and constituency
- Environmental justice organizations (especially anti-petrochemical groups)
- Housing rights advocates (tenant organizations, anti-gentrification groups)
- Legal advocacy organizations
- UPR faculty and student organizations
- Labor unions aligned with progressive politics
- Feminist organizations (Coordinadora Paz para la Mujer and similar)

## National/International Connections

- Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (AOC): Puerto Rican congresswoman in Washington; ideological alignment; has amplified PR progressive voices including Alianza positions
- U.S. progressive network: Bernie Sanders wing of Democratic Party has expressed solidarity with PR independence/progressive forces
- International: PIP's traditional international connections extend to Nogales through the Alianza
