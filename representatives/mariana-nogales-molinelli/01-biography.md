## Professional Career Before Politics

### Legal Career
- Practiced law before entering politics
- Civil rights and constitutional law orientation (consistent with her political positions)
- Legal advocacy work: represented clients and causes in the progressive/civil rights space
- Her legal background gives her the ability to read legislation in real time and explain complex legal language to general audiences — her signature political skill

### Academic/Advocacy Work
- Involved in academic and advocacy circles at UPR and in San Juan's progressive intellectual community
- Connected to LGBTQ+ advocacy organizations
- Environmental and housing justice advocacy

## Path to Politics

### 2016: First Run (Comisionada Residente — PIP)
- Ran for Resident Commissioner (Puerto Rico's non-voting delegate to the U.S. Congress) under the PIP banner
- Did not win, but established herself as a credible progressive voice
- The campaign built name recognition and connected her to PIP's organizational network

### 2020: Election to Cámara por Acumulación (MVC/PIP Alliance)
- Ran for Representative por Acumulación under the emerging MVC coalition aligned with PIP
- The political moment: post-Telegramgate (2019), post-COVID-19, mass movement energy
- Won a seat — part of MVC's historic 2020 breakthrough
- Became one of the most visible freshman legislators in PR history almost immediately

### Why She Rose So Fast
1. **Social media fluency:** Started explaining legislation on Instagram in real time — viral immediately
2. **Legal credibility:** She could read the bills and explain what they actually said, not what politicians claimed
3. **Clear ideology:** Not trying to be all things; clear progressive values
4. **Authenticity:** Didn't look or sound like a typical PR politician
5. **LGBTQ+ allyship:** Filled a genuine gap in PR's legislative representation

## Personal Narrative She Tells
Nogales presents herself as someone who went into law and advocacy because she believed in justice, and entered politics because the legislature needed people who would actually read the laws and represent communities that had been invisible in the system. Her story is not one of political ambition in the traditional sense — it's framed as moral obligation.

## Community Before Politics
- Connected to San Juan's progressive, artistic, and legal community
- Part of the professional network that was energized by the Verano del 19 protests
- Her entry into politics was a natural extension of pre-existing advocacy work rather than a career pivot
