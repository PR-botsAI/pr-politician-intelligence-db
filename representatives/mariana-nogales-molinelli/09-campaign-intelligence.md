# Campaign Intelligence — Mariana Nogales Molinelli

## 2020 Campaign

**Strategy:** MVC's inaugural campaign; digital-first due to COVID-19 pandemic restricting in-person events
- Social media organizing replaced traditional rallies
- Nogales' pre-existing network and emerging social media presence drove turnout among young progressive voters
- Small-dollar fundraising model — intentionally avoided corporate donations
- Authenticity as strategy: no slick production, direct-to-camera communication, genuine

**What worked:**
- Verano del 19 energy channeled into electoral participation
- Digital organizing reached voters who don't engage with traditional politics
- MVC's brand as genuinely new and different from PNP/PPD
- Nogales personally: her legal credibility + transparency + LGBTQ+ advocacy = distinct profile

## 2024 Re-election Campaign

**Strategy:** Alianza de País (MVC + PIP) joint campaign; Dalmau as gubernatorial candidate
- Coalition strategy: pool resources, avoid vote-splitting
- Constitutional Minority Clause: knowing PNP would win supermajority, Alianza positioned to maximize minority seats guaranteed by the clause
- Nogales' re-election was never in serious doubt — her personal following secured the MVC at-large seats

**Vote data:** Research 2024 certified results on ceepur.org for MVC at-large totals

## Funding Model

- Small donor focused — philosophically consistent with anti-corporate political stance
- Social media fundraising: Instagram/Twitter appeals during key moments
- Community events (when held) as fundraising vehicles
- Does not have access to PNP/PPD party machine fundraising
- Likely relies on diaspora donations from Puerto Ricans in the U.S. mainland who share her politics

## Digital Strategy Analysis

**What she has:**
- Large organic Instagram following (among largest of any PR legislator)
- High Twitter/X engagement
- Earned media through her accountability work — she generates news
- Diaspora reach

**What she doesn't have:**
- A lead capture system tied to her campaigns
- An email list of any scale (likely)
- A CRM of supporters organized by municipality, issue, volunteer status
- A WhatsApp broadcast system for organized outreach
- Donation processing infrastructure beyond basic

**This is our exact pitch point:**
"You have the reach. You need the infrastructure to turn reach into organized political power."

## 2028 Outlook

- She will run for re-election (or potentially higher office)
- The Alianza de País will likely continue and may expand
- Key strategic question: Can MVC/Alianza grow from ~15% to 20%+ to become a genuine swing factor or minority partner in governance?
- Nogales' personal brand will be central to any MVC growth strategy
- A robust digital infrastructure built now = a decisive advantage in 2028
