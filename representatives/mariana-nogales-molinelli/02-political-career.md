## Electoral History

### 2016 — Resident Commissioner Race (PIP)
- **Office:** Resident Commissioner of Puerto Rico (Washington, D.C.)
- **Result:** Lost
- **Significance:** Demonstrated willingness to run, built PIP/progressive network connections
- **Context:** Long-shot race; PIP rarely wins island-wide races

### 2020 — Representative por Acumulación (MVC)
- **Office:** C ámara de Representantes, At-Large seat
- **Result:** WON — part of MVC's 2020 breakthrough
- **Context:** First election as an MVC-affiliated candidate; MVC entered as new party
- **Vote base:** San Juan metropolitan area, university communities, progressive urban voters
- **Turnout driver:** The Verano del 19 energy; COVID-era digital campaigning

### 2024 — Re-election por Acumulación (Alianza de País)
- **Office:** Same at-large seat
- **Result:** WON — re-elected as part of Alianza de País coalition (MVC + PIP)
- **Context:** PNP won supermajority; Alianza gained additional minority seats via Constitutional Clause
- **Significance:** Her re-election confirmed she had a durable base, not just a one-cycle phenomenon

## Most Notable Legislative Actions

### "Leamos el Proyecto" (Let's Read the Bill)
Nogales made a habit of live-reading and explaining legislation on Instagram — catching provisions that other legislators had either not read or hoped the public wouldn't notice. This became her signature contribution to PR political culture. Examples:
- Called out provisions in budget bills that quietly redirected funds
- Explained PROMESA-related legislation in accessible language
- Read LUMA Energy contract provisions to her followers
- Flagged anti-LGBTQ+ language embedded in broader legislation

### Anti-Corruption Votes and Statements
- Voted against legislation she found problematic even when procedurally difficult
- Called out government contracts and procurement irregularities
- Named names when necessary — not diplomatic in the traditional sense

### LGBTQ+ Advocacy
- Introduced or co-sponsored legislation protecting LGBTQ+ rights
- Spoke on the floor advocating for LGBTQ+ community visibility
- Has confronted colleagues who made homophobic statements

## Career Trajectory
- Nogales is one of the few PR politicians whose ambitions could reasonably extend to higher office
- Her at-large base is island-wide; she's not limited by a single district
- Possible future path: Senate por Acumulación, higher MVC/Alianza leadership role
- The 2028 election cycle will determine whether MVC/Alianza can grow toward being a genuine governing force
- She is the Alianza's most valuable political asset
