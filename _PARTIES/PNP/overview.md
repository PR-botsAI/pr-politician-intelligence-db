## Founding & Origin
The PNP was founded on August 20, 1967, by Luis A. Ferré, a wealthy industrialist and art patron from Ponce, following the 1967 plebiscite on Puerto Rico's status. Ferré had previously been active in the Republican Statehood Party and saw the need for a dedicated statehood movement. The party drew its early support from the business community, professionals, and Catholics who favored modernization and integration with the United States.

Ferré won the governorship in 1968, becoming the first PNP governor, defeating the long-dominant PPD. This established the two-party dynamic that has defined PR politics ever since.
