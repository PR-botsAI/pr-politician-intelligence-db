# PNP — Internal Party Structure

## Leadership Hierarchy

The PNP operates as a hierarchical party with a strong centralized leadership at the top and a robust municipal machine at the bottom.

### Top Leadership
| Position | Current Holder | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Party President | Jenniffer González Colón | Also Governor; party president = de facto supreme leader |
| Senate President | Thomas Rivera Schatz | 3rd term; most powerful legislative figure in PR |
| House Speaker | Carlos "Johnny" Méndez Núñez | 2nd term; manages House supermajority |
| Resident Commissioner (2025-) | Vacant/PPD | Pablo Hernández (PPD) won; PNP does not control this |

### Internal Factions

**The Jenniffer Wing (Moderate Statehooders)**
Aligned with Governor González Colón. Pragmatic, executive-focused, works within federal system effectively. Prioritizes governance results over ideological purity. Close to national Republican Party establishment. Represents the professional/suburban PNP base.

**The Schatz Wing (Aggressive Statehooders)**
Aligned with Senate President Rivera Schatz. More confrontational governing style. Known for aggressive use of legislative power. "El Toro" faction. Willing to use supermajority muscle without compromise. More hardline on status.

**The Old Guard**
Veteran PNP figures with roots in the Rosselló and Fortuño eras. Some tension with newer generation. Patronage network holders. Key in municipal machine.

**The Reformers**
Smaller faction; younger PNP members who want to modernize party image post-Telegramgate. Less visible publicly but pushing for ethics reforms internally.

## How PNP Nominates Candidates

### Primary System
- Puerto Rico holds open primaries
- PNP primary is the most competitive internal process
- High-profile primaries can be brutal — Rivera Schatz's Senate career included primary battles
- 2024: Most primaries were uncontested (party unified behind incumbents)
- Signature collection required before primary (50% by January, 100% by February deadline)

### At-Large vs. District
- District candidates: run in specific geographic districts
- At-large candidates: compete island-wide for 11 Senate and 11 House at-large seats
- At-large races are more dependent on party machinery and name recognition than local service

## Municipal Machine

The PNP's 37 mayors (2024) form a critical second tier of power:
- Municipal governments control thousands of municipal jobs
- Public works contracts flow through municipal offices
- Mayors deliver voter turnout on election day through local networks
- Even when PNP loses the governorship, municipal mayors keep the machine alive
- Municipal assemblies (legislatures) are also elected — PNP controls many

### Patronage Networks
The patronage system (cunas, botellas — jobs given for political loyalty) operates at both state and municipal level. It is practiced by PNP and PPD equally. Public employees understand that a change in administration can mean job uncertainty. This creates strong incentive for party loyalty.

## Relationship with U.S. Republican Party

- PNP officially aligns with the Republican Party nationally
- Governor González Colón is a Republican who caucuses with the House Republican Conference as Resident Commissioner
- She has maintained relationships with Republican leadership while occasionally criticizing policies that hurt PR (disaster relief, Medicaid)
- The statehood argument is bipartisan (Democrats also support statehood legislation) but PNP is structurally Republican
- Tension: National Republican Party's ambivalence about Puerto Rican statehood (adding 5+ Democratic-leaning congressional seats)
- Post-Telegramgate (2019 Rosselló resignation): PNP had to rebuild relationship with national GOP

---

## Party Discipline

- Under Rivera Schatz's Senate leadership: very tight discipline — votes rarely break with party
- Defectors face consequences: loss of committee assignments, primary challenges
- The supermajority (2024) makes discipline easier — majority is so large that individual defections don't matter
- González Colón uses executive appointments and contracts as discipline tools (standard PR politics)

---

## Post-Telegramgate Restructuring (2019-2025)

The 2019 resignation of Governor Ricardo Rosselló after the Telegramgate scandal (916 pages of private chat showing misogyny, homophobia, corruption jokes) forced PNP to:
- Distance itself from Rosselló's personal brand
- Navigate succession crisis (Wanda Vázquez became governor 2019-2021)
- Rebuild public trust
- Jenniffer González Colón's 2024 re-election with expanded supermajority shows full recovery
- Rosselló is now persona non grata in official PNP circles
