# PNP — Voter Base Profile

## Who Votes PNP

### Demographic Breakdown

**Age:** PNP has historically been stronger with middle-aged voters (35–64). Post-2020, younger voters have become more split, with some gravitating to MVC. Older voters (65+) are divided between PNP and PPD depending on region.

**Income:** PNP performs well across income levels but particularly with:
- Middle-income households ($25K–$60K)
- Business owners and self-employed professionals
- Government contractors
- Military families and veterans

**Education:** PNP does well with college-educated professionals (lawyers, engineers, doctors, business people) who see statehood as path to economic parity.

**Religion:** Historically Catholic, but PNP draws from all religious groups. Less dependent on evangelical base than PD; more moderate Catholic is core.

**Occupation:**
- Private sector employees
- Business owners
- Medical and legal professionals
- Military and veterans
- Some public sector (though PPD is stronger with public unions)

---

## Geographic Strongholds

**Urban/Suburban:** PNP is strongest in the San Juan metropolitan area (San Juan, Bayamón, Guaynabo, Carolina, Caguas corridor). These are the most populated, most economically developed areas of PR.

**Northern Coast:** Arecibo area, Manatí, Barceloneta — pharmaceutical manufacturing belt that benefits from U.S. economic integration.

**Eastern PR:** Humacao area, Fajardo corridor.

**Municipalities won in 2024 (37):** Includes major population centers.

**PPD Territory:** Interior mountain municipalities (Lares, Adjuntas, Jayuya) and western PR (some) trend PPD.

## Why They Vote PNP: The Core Arguments

### The Economic Argument
"As a state, we would receive $10+ billion more annually in federal funds (Medicaid parity, SSI, food stamps at state rates). Statehood = economic equality."

### The Dignity/Rights Argument
"We are American citizens. We serve in the military. We pay federal taxes when we move to a state. We deserve full voting representation. It is a matter of civil rights and democratic equality."

### The Pragmatic Argument
"Independence is economically impossible given our dependency on federal programs. The ELA hasn't evolved. Statehood is the only realistic path to a better future."

### The Cultural Reconciliation
"Hawaii and New Mexico are states with distinct cultures, different languages, different histories. We can be Puerto Rican AND American. Statehood doesn't erase our identity."

## What Moves PNP Voters Emotionally

- **Pride in American citizenship + Puerto Rican identity** — the dual identity that PNP politicians must embody
- **Frustration at being treated as second-class** — federal program disparities, no presidential vote, no congressional vote
- **Optimism** — statehood as path to a better Puerto Rico, not just political status
- **Safety and order** — PNP tends to be stronger on law-and-order messaging
- **Economic development** — private investment, jobs, modernization
- **Family stability** — traditional family values (moderate, not as intense as PD)

---

## Generational Shifts

**Baby Boomers (born 1946–1964):** Strong PNP loyalty. Experienced the development era. Remember Fortuño's austerity but also Rosselló's growth years.

**Gen X (born 1965–1980):** Divided. Experienced economic decline, emigration pressures. Some stayed PNP, some shifted to PPD or MVC.

**Millennials (born 1981–1996):** Most competitive generation. PNP competes for these voters against MVC specifically. The Telegramgate scandal hit PNP credibility with this group.

**Gen Z (born 1997–2012):** Most competitive. Many gravitate to MVC's progressive messaging. PNP needs to modernize to capture these voters.

---

## Messaging That Works with PNP Base
- Competence and professionalism in government
- Concrete results (infrastructure built, federal money secured)
- Statehood as economic justice
- Puerto Rican pride compatible with American citizenship
- Safety and law and order
- "El progreso" — progress as tangible improvement in daily life
