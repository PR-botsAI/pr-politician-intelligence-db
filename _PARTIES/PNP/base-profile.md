# PNP — Voter Base Profile

## Who Votes PNP

### Demographics
- **Age:** Skews slightly older but spans all ages; younger PNP voters are growing
- **Geography:** Urban and suburban — San Juan metro, north coast, Bayamón corridor
- **Income:** Spans working class to professional/business class
- **Education:** Moderate-to-high — business professionals, government workers, military families
- **Religion:** Predominantly Catholic; some evangelical crossover

### Psychographics
- **Identity:** "Proud American AND proud Puerto Rican" — sees no contradiction
- **Values:** Stability, progress, integration, economic opportunity through U.S. relationship
- **Fears:** Economic isolation (independence), cultural loss (manageable), being second-class citizens (statehood resolves)
- **Motivators:** Federal parity, voting rights, economic development through U.S. investment

### The PNP Base is NOT Monolithic
- **Business PNP:** Supports statehood for economic stability; pro-investment, pro-Act 60
- **Military PNP:** Veterans and military families who feel deeply American
- **Government worker PNP:** Want federal pay equity and pension security that statehood would bring
- **Aspirational PNP:** Young professionals who see statehood as economic modernization
- **Inherited PNP:** Families that have voted PNP for generations — tribal loyalty

## What PNP Voters WANT FROM Their Legislators
1. Statehood advocacy (the mission)
2. Local services delivered (roads, water, safety)
3. Access to federal programs
4. Economic development (jobs, investment)
5. Accountability and results — Rosselló burned trust; they want clean governance

## What PNP Voters FEAR
- PPD returning to power and slowing statehood progress
- Corruption scandals (Telegramgate PTSD)
- Economic decline
- Federal government abandonment of PR
