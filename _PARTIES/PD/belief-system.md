# Proyecto Dignidad — Belief System & Ideology

## Core Identity
Proyecto Dignidad is Puerto Rico's Christian conservative party, governing explicitly from a faith-based framework. It applies evangelical and natural law principles to political positions. Status is NOT the party's primary identity — this distinguishes it from all other PR parties.

---

## Theological and Philosophical Foundation

- **Natural law tradition:** The belief that certain moral truths are accessible to reason and embedded in creation; government should conform to these truths
- **Christian anthropology:** Human dignity comes from being made in God's image; policy must protect this dignity from conception to natural death
- **Traditional family:** Marriage is between one man and one woman; the family (not the state) is the primary social unit
- **Subsidiarity:** Government should operate at the lowest effective level; family and community before government

---

## Key Issue Positions

### Abortion
- **Position:** Pro-life from conception
- **Intensity:** HIGHEST — this is a non-negotiable core commitment
- **Policy:** Opposes abortion in virtually all circumstances; advocates for legal protection of unborn life
- **Frame:** Abortion as the defining human rights issue of our time

### LGBTQ+ Rights
- **Position:** Opposes same-sex marriage, opposes adoption by same-sex couples, opposes gender transition interventions especially for minors
- **Intensity:** High — this is a defining issue for the party's base
- **Frame:** "Protecting children," "defending natural family," "freedom of conscience for religious institutions"
- **Note:** PD does not advocate for criminalizing homosexuality; their position is against state recognition and celebration of same-sex relationships

### Education
- **Position:** Parental rights in education; school choice/vouchers; religious education
- **Opposes:** Teaching of "gender ideology" in public schools
- **Supports:** Homeschooling rights; private/religious school funding

### Economic Policy
- **Position:** Free market, pro-small business, anti-government dependency
- **Supports:** Personal responsibility; private sector solutions
- **Critique:** Government welfare as creating dependency that undermines family and dignity

### Political Status
- **Position:** Not the primary issue; PD says Puerto Ricans should decide status through a legitimate process but this is not PD's defining issue
- **Practical:** PD has some statehood-sympathetic members and some non-status members; intentionally avoids making status its identity

### Religious Freedom
- **Position:** Churches, religious organizations, and individuals should not be compelled to violate their religious beliefs
- **Specific concerns:** Pastors forced to perform same-sex weddings; adoption agencies forced to place with same-sex couples; Christian business owners forced to serve same-sex wedding events

---

## The PD Voter's Worldview

"Puerto Rico has lost its way morally. Abortion has been legalized, same-sex marriage imposed by federal courts without our consent, and our children are being taught things in schools that contradict our faith. The other parties accept or celebrate this. We need a party that governs from the truth of God's design for human life and family."

---

## Tension Within PD

- Freedom of religion vs. LGBTQ+ anti-discrimination law: constant legal battleground
- Economic policy: PD's free market position can conflict with its base's dependency on federal programs (many evangelical communities in PR are economically vulnerable)
- Electoral math: PD alone cannot win the governorship; must eventually make alliances, which requires compromising some positions
