# Proyecto Dignidad — Voter Base Profile

## Who Votes PD

### The Evangelical Community
PD's primary base is Puerto Rico's evangelical Protestant community:
- Estimated 30–35% of PR population identifies as evangelical
- Politically active: evangelical churches function as community organizing hubs
- Theologically conservative: complement their faith with political engagement
- Pastors often encourage political participation along conservative lines
- Many are first or second-generation converts from Catholicism

### Theologically Conservative Catholics
Some traditional Catholics who feel the other parties have conceded too much on life and family issues:
- More common in older age groups
- May vote PD in legislative races while voting PNP/PPD in gubernatorial races

### Geographic Distribution
- Evangelical churches are found throughout PR, not concentrated in one region
- Stronger presence in: Pentecostal communities (strong in urban working class), some interior municipalities, rapidly growing suburban evangelical congregations
- NOT a geographic party: it's a religious community party, distributed across the island

---

## Age and Demographic Profile

- **Age:** 35–65 is PD's strongest age cohort. Older evangelicals who have been in the church for decades.
- **Gender:** Conservative faith communities often have engaged women voters; PD may over-index with religiously conservative women
- **Income:** Mixed; evangelical community in PR includes working class to middle class
- **Education:** Mixed; does not correlate strongly with education level

---

## What Drives PD Loyalty

1. **Faith identity over party identity:** PD voters vote for their values, not a party machine
2. **Single-issue intensity:** Abortion and LGBTQ+ issues are high-enough priorities that voters will switch parties to vote against them
3. **Community cohesion:** The evangelical church network means PD voters are connected to each other through existing community structures
4. **Pastor influence:** Pastoral recommendations carry significant weight in evangelical communities
5. **Moral clarity:** In a political landscape seen as corrupt and unprincipled, PD offers clarity: "We govern from God's truth"

---

## Electoral Ceiling and Limitation

PD's ceiling is approximately the evangelical vote percentage minus those who prioritize other issues:
- If 30% evangelical, perhaps 15–20% would vote PD primarily
- That's a significant base for legislative seats but not sufficient for the governorship without alliance
- PD won some legislative seats in 2020 and 2024 — the party is a real legislative force even if not an executive one
