# MVC — Internal Party Structure

## How a Movement Became a Party

MVC's organizational DNA is different from PNP or PPD. It emerged from civil society — community organizations, activist networks, legal advocacy groups — rather than from traditional patronage or electoral machines. This gives it both a strength (authentic grassroots energy) and a weakness (less institutional infrastructure).

### Core Organizational Principle
MVC operates on a flatter, more horizontal model than the hierarchical PNP/PPD. Decision-making involves broader consultation with local chapters and allied organizations. The party sees itself as a movement with an electoral arm, not a traditional political party.

### Manuel Natal's Role
Natal is the organizational backbone. He left the PPD, where he was a Representative, after feeling the party was too ideologically limited. He brought legislative experience, institutional knowledge, and a network of progressive community organizations when he founded MVC. He functions as the party's chief organizer and strategic voice.

### Mariana Nogales as Brand
Nogales is MVC's public face and social media engine. Her ability to communicate complex legislative issues to a mass audience is MVC's most valuable political asset. She functions almost as MVC's brand ambassador through her personal following, which exceeds the party's institutional following.

## The Alianza de País (MVC + PIP Coalition)

For the 2024 election, MVC and PIP (Partido Independentista Puertorriqueño) formed the Alianza de País — a formal electoral coalition:
- Shared ballot line in some races
- Joint campaign events
- Agreement on candidate distribution to avoid vote splitting
- Juan Dalmau (PIP) ran as the Alianza's gubernatorial candidate
- Both parties maintained separate identities while sharing resources

**Why the alliance works ideologically:**
- Both favor decolonization framework over statehood or ELA
- Both are left-progressive on economic issues
- Both oppose LUMA, PROMESA board overreach, Act 60
- PIP brings historical depth and cultural credibility; MVC brings youth energy and social media reach

**Tension in the alliance:**
- PIP is explicitly pro-independence; MVC is more ambiguous
- Some MVC voters are not independence supporters
- Alliance requires managing different voter expectations

## Candidate Selection

- More participatory process than PNP/PPD
- Local chapters have input
- Emphasis on candidates with community organizing backgrounds
- Resists traditional political career types ("career politicians")
- This produces more authentic candidates but sometimes less electorally experienced ones

---

## Fundraising Model

- Small-donor focused — intentionally avoids large corporate donors
- Digital fundraising through social media appeals
- Community events as fundraising vehicles
- Cannot match PNP/PPD institutional fundraising but compensates with lower overhead and volunteer energy
- MVC's model is closer to U.S. progressive campaigns (Bernie Sanders-style small dollar) than traditional PR machine politics

---

## Community Organizing as Infrastructure

MVC's substitute for the patronage machine is community organizing networks:
- Legal aid organizations
- Environmental justice groups
- Housing advocacy organizations
- LGBTQ+ advocacy groups
- Labor organizing networks
- University student organizations
- These allied organizations provide volunteer labor, communication networks, and credibility
