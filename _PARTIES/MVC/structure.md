## The Alianza de País (MVC + PIP Coalition)

In 2020 and 2024, MVC and PIP ran as an electoral coalition — **Alianza de País**.

**Why the alliance works:**
- Both are decolonization/progressive parties
- Neither can win alone — together they maximize legislative seats
- Constitutional Minority Clause gives them more seats when PNP wins supermajority
- Different strengths: MVC has more candidates; PIP has historical brand

**How it works:**
- Joint candidate lists for at-large (por acumulación) seats
- Coordination on district races to avoid splitting the vote
- Shared caucus in legislature

## How MVC Operates Differently

### Movement-Party Hybrid
- MVC blurs the line between political party and social movement
- Its politicians show up at marches, protests, community organizing events
- Legislative office is treated as platform for movement, not just governance

### Transparency Culture
- MVC legislators publish their schedules, votes, explanations publicly
- Mariana Nogales is famous for explaining every vote on social media
- This transparency is both a value and a differentiation strategy

### Social Media Native
- MVC was founded in the social media era and uses it natively
- Instagram, Twitter, YouTube are primary organizing tools
- Live-streaming legislative proceedings with commentary
- Direct constituent engagement in comments
