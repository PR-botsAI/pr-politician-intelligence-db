## Origin & Context

MVC was born from the political upheaval of 2019 — the year of the "Verano del 19" (Summer of 2019), when mass protests in Puerto Rico forced Governor Ricardo Rosselló to resign after the "Telegramgate" scandal (racist, sexist private messages were leaked).

The party emerged from a generation of Puerto Ricans — particularly young, educated, urban, progressive — who felt that neither the PNP nor the PPD represented them. The founding figures were community organizers, lawyers, social activists, and academics who had long worked outside traditional party structures.

Alexandra Lúgaro, an independent candidate who had run strong campaigns in 2016 and 2020, is closely associated with the movement though not formally a party member. Manuel Natal Albelo, a former PPD representative who broke with the party, became one of MVC's key figures.

- **Alexandra Lúgaro** — Independent aligned, ran for governor in 2016 (independent) and 2020 (MVC)
- **José Vargas Vidot** — Senator, known for work with homeless and marginalized communities
