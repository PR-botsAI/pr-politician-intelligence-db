# MVC — Belief System & Ideology

## Core Identity
MVC is Puerto Rico's principal left-progressive movement, combining anti-colonialism, social justice, economic reform, and grassroots democracy. It draws from multiple progressive traditions: the Latin American left, U.S. progressive politics, feminist theory, environmental justice, and community organizing.

---

## Political Status Position

- MVC does not formally endorse independence but operates within a **decolonization framework**
- Partners with PIP (Partido Independentista) in the Alianza de País coalition
- The shared frame is: **the current colonial relationship must end** — the path (independence vs. other) is debated
- Many MVC members personally favor independence
- Official position: self-determination through a democratic process, not imposed by either the U.S. or party machinery
- **Rejects both statehood (PNP) and traditional ELA (PPD)** as perpetuating colonial dependency

---

## Economic Ideology

### Core Framework: Social Democracy with Anti-Colonial Edge

- **Anti-austerity, anti-PROMESA:** Strongly opposes the Fiscal Oversight Board as a colonial imposition
- **Debt audit:** Advocates for an audit of Puerto Rico's public debt before paying it
- **Anti-privatization:** Fiercely opposes LUMA Energy, private contractors in public services
- **Pro-public sector:** Supports public ownership of utilities, transportation, healthcare
- **Workers' rights:** Strongly pro-union, pro-labor protections
- **Living wage:** Advocates for significant minimum wage increases
- **Universal basic services:** Healthcare, housing, education as rights
- **Anti-Act 60:** Opposes tax incentives for wealthy investors ("los millonarios" who don't pay taxes)
- **Community economies:** Supports cooperatives, local food systems, mutual aid networks

### PROMESA / Fiscal Board
MVC is the most consistently anti-PROMESA party. They frame the Fiscal Board as:
- A violation of Puerto Rico's self-governance
- An instrument of U.S. colonial control
- A mechanism for imposing austerity on working people to pay bondholders

## Social Ideology

### LGBTQ+ Rights
- MVC is the most explicitly pro-LGBTQ+ party in Puerto Rico
- Mariana Nogales is openly LGBTQ+-allied and vocal on this
- Supports marriage equality, trans rights, anti-discrimination protections
- This positions them clearly left of PNP and PPD on social issues

### Feminism & Gender Justice
- Strong feminist framework throughout the party
- Several female leaders: Nogales, Lúgaro, others
- Advocates for reproductive rights, gender-based violence protections
- Tracks femicide data, advocates for emergency response to gender violence

### Racial Justice
- Afro-Puerto Rican identity and rights — more explicitly discussed than in PNP/PPD
- Anti-racism explicitly part of platform
- Addresses disproportionate poverty and exclusion of Afro-Puerto Ricans

### Housing & Gentrification
- Very vocal on the Act 60/20/22 investor incentive programs that they argue are displacing Puerto Ricans
- Advocates for rent control, tenant protections
- Opposes luxury development without affordable housing requirements

### Environment
- Strong environmental platform
- Opposes petrochemical expansion
- Advocates for 100% renewable energy
- Connects environmental justice with economic justice and colonial exploitation

---

## Governance Philosophy

- **Grassroots democracy:** The party emerged from protest movements; maintains that energy
- **Transparency:** Strong anti-corruption, open government positions
- **Participatory governance:** Supports public participation in policy decisions
- **Anti-establishment:** Defines itself against the PNP/PPD duopoly
- **Movement-party hybrid:** Blurs line between political party and social movement

---

## Base & Coalition

- **Youth (18–35):** Strongest generational base, especially university students
- **Urban progressives:** San Juan metro area, Santurce, Rio Piedras
- **Artists, academics, intellectuals**
- **LGBTQ+ community**
- **Environmental activists**
- **Labor activists**
- **Diaspora Puerto Ricans** who come back or influence from U.S.

---

## Rhetorical Patterns

### Key Words
"El pueblo" · "Descolonización" · "Justicia" · "Dignidad" · "Los de abajo" · "Resistencia" · "Comunidad" · "Transparencia"

### Emotional Frame
- Moral urgency: This is a matter of justice, not just politics
- Solidarity with the marginalized
- Critique of the powerful (business class, party bosses, U.S. Congress)
- Hope and collective action
- Pride in Puerto Rican identity that doesn't require U.S. integration

### Communication Style
- Most digitally fluent party
- Uses Instagram, Twitter aggressively
- Long-form explanations of issues (especially Nogales)
- Hashtag campaigns, viral moments
- Conversational with constituents, not bureaucratic
