# MVC — Party History

## The 2019 Context — El Verano del 19

MVC cannot be understood without understanding the summer of 2019.

**July 2019:** ~900 pages of private Telegram messages from Governor Ricardo Rosselló's inner circle were leaked. The messages included:
- Racist jokes about the Puerto Rican diaspora
- Misogynistic comments about women politicians and activists
- Callous jokes about Hurricane Maria victims
- Homophobic language
- Discussion of political opposition using crude and violent imagery

**The reaction:** Puerto Rico exploded. Over 500,000 people — in a population of ~3.2 million — took to the streets. For 12 days straight, massive protests at La Fortaleza (governor's mansion). It was the largest protest in Puerto Rican history.

**Rosselló resigned** July 24, 2019 — the first Puerto Rican governor to resign.

**What this created:**
- A generation of Puerto Ricans who believed mass action works
- A political vacuum on the left — who would channel this energy?
- The conditions for MVC's founding and rapid growth

## MVC's Founding (2019)

Manuel Natal Albelo (PPD representative who became increasingly frustrated with PPD's machine politics) and others founded MVC in 2019 to channel the Verano del 19 energy into electoral politics.

Key founding principles:
- Citizen power over party machines
- Transparency and anti-corruption
- Progressive values (economic justice, LGBTQ+ rights, environment)
- Decolonization framework
- New generation politics

## 2020: First Election — Exceeds Expectations
- MVC fielded candidates as Alianza de País (with PIP)
- Won seats in both Senate and House — immediately relevant
- Mariana Nogales won her at-large representative seat
- Alexandra Lúgaro won 13.5% as governor candidate — remarkable for new party
- Established MVC as the viable third force

## 2024: Consolidation
- Re-elected MVC/Alianza legislators
- Gained additional seats via Constitutional Minority Clause
- Growing municipal presence
- National attention on Mariana Nogales (featured in U.S. media)
