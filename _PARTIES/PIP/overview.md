## Founding & Historical Context

The PIP was founded in 1946 as a breakaway from the PPD. Founders felt that Luis Muñoz Marín's decision to pursue Commonwealth (ELA) instead of independence was a betrayal of Puerto Rico's national aspirations. The party has run candidates in every election since 1948.

### FBI Persecution (COINTELPRO)
The PIP was subjected to FBI surveillance and infiltration for decades. The Puerto Rico government kept **"carpetas"** — political surveillance files — on tens of thousands of independence supporters, including professors, artists, lawyers, and activists. This history of state repression is part of the party's identity and creates a powerful narrative of resistance.

The carpetas were exposed in 1987 and the files are now public. This history explains why independence supporters in PR have a distinct culture of resistance and why many view the U.S. government with deep suspicion.

### The Pedro Albizu Campos Legacy
Albizu Campos (1891–1965) is the iconic figure of Puerto Rican independence — not formally PIP but deeply associated. Harvard lawyer, Nationalist Party leader, imprisoned multiple times by U.S. government, he represents the radical independence tradition that PIP inherits spiritually.
