# PIP — Belief System & Ideology

## Core Identity
The PIP holds the most ideologically coherent position in Puerto Rican politics: **full sovereignty and independence** for Puerto Rico as a matter of decolonization, national self-determination, and justice. It is the only major party whose entire identity is built around a single, consistent principle.

---

## The Independence Doctrine

### Philosophical Foundation
- Puerto Rico is a **nation** — with a distinct language, culture, history, and identity
- The colonial relationship with the U.S. is fundamentally illegitimate regardless of its economic benefits
- Self-determination is a right under international law (UN resolutions)
- No dependency relationship, however comfortable, is acceptable for a people with national consciousness
- The colonial relationship deforms Puerto Rican society, economy, and culture

### Practical Vision
- An independent Puerto Rico would:
  - Control its own trade agreements
  - Have full UN membership and diplomatic relations
  - Manage its own economy without Fiscal Board interference
  - Control immigration, currency, and monetary policy
  - Be free to develop its own economic model

### On U.S. Citizenship
- The PIP's position: U.S. citizenship should be available to those who want it post-independence (transitional)
- But Puerto Ricans' primary citizenship would be Puerto Rican
- Many PIP supporters already consider themselves Puerto Rican first, American incidentally

### The Economic Counter-Argument
Opponents say independence would be economically disastrous (loss of federal funds, Medicaid, food stamps). PIP's response:
- Federal funds are tools of dependency that distort Puerto Rico's economy
- Independence nations develop their own economic capacities
- Cuba, Costa Rica, other Caribbean nations are independent and functional
- The colonial relationship has not solved PR's economic crisis anyway

---

## Economic Ideology

- **Democratic socialism** or social democracy tradition
- Anti-neoliberal: opposes IMF-style austerity, privatization
- Pro-cooperative economy
- Advocates for land reform, community ownership
- Anti-debt payment to bondholders who engaged in predatory lending
- Advocates for economic diversification away from pharmaceutical/manufacturing dependency
- Pro-agriculture revival (food sovereignty)
- Strongly anti-Act 60 investor incentives

---

## Cultural Ideology

- Spanish is the language of Puerto Rico — must be protected and elevated
- Puerto Rican culture (music, art, literature, jibaro tradition) is to be preserved and celebrated
- Afro-Puerto Rican heritage acknowledged and honored
- Indigenous (Taíno) heritage acknowledged
- Anti-cultural assimilation into U.S. mainstream culture
- The PIP has historically attracted artists, writers, musicians — the cultural intelligentsia

---

## Rhetorical Patterns

### Key Words
"Independencia" · "Nación" · "Autodeterminación" · "Pueblo soberano" · "Descolonización" · "Patria" · "Justicia"

### Emotional Frame
- Historical justice for a colonized people
- Pride in national identity untethered from the colonizer
- Moral clarity: colonialism is wrong, period
- Resistance tradition — we have survived persecution, we will prevail
- Hope for a truly sovereign future

### The PIP Voter's Mindset
- Intellectually committed to independence as a matter of principle
- Often has higher education level than average voter
- Sees the status question through a decolonization/human rights lens
- Skeptical of both PNP and PPD as parties of the colonial system
- Activist-oriented: sees voting as part of a broader movement
