# PIP — Messaging Patterns & Communication

## The PIP Communication Challenge

PIP's core challenge is messaging independence in a way that doesn't trigger economic fear. The majority of Puerto Ricans depend on federal programs (Medicaid, food stamps/SNAP, SSI, Section 8) that would be at risk in independence. PIP must acknowledge this reality while making the affirmative case.

---

## Core Messaging Strategies

### Strategy 1: Decolonization Frame (Not "Independence")
PIP increasingly uses "descolonización" rather than "independencia" as the lead frame. Decolonization is framed as a right, as a process, as justice — not as an immediate economic leap. This buys rhetorical space to discuss the path without triggering the "we'll lose our benefits" reflex.

### Strategy 2: PROMESA as Colonial Proof
"The Fiscal Oversight Board was imposed by the U.S. Congress without Puerto Rico's consent, overriding our constitution, cutting our pensions, making decisions that affect our lives. THIS is what the colonial relationship looks like. The ELA didn't protect us. Statehood hasn't arrived. Only independence gives us control of our own decisions."

### Strategy 3: Economic Sovereignty Arguments
"An independent Puerto Rico would control its own trade agreements. We could negotiate with Caribbean nations, with Latin America. We wouldn't have to use the Jones Act (1920) which forces us to use expensive U.S. ships for shipping — making everything more expensive. We would have our own monetary policy."

### Strategy 4: The Comparison to Other Caribbean Nations
"Dominican Republic: independent. Trinidad: independent. Barbados: recently became a republic. Jamaica: independent. These nations manage their own affairs. Are they perfect? No. But they are sovereign. Why can't we be?"

### Strategy 5: Juan Dalmau's Personal Style
Dalmau communicates independence in accessible, conversational, sometimes humorous ways. He doesn't lecture; he talks. He acknowledges economic fears directly and responds to them rather than dismissing them. This humanizes the independence argument.

## Key Phrases
- **"La independencia es el camino"** — Independence is the way
- **"Autodeterminación"** — Self-determination
- **"Colonia"** — PIP uses this word proudly; it's their most honest frame
- **"La Junta no es nuestra"** — The [Fiscal] Board is not ours (imposed, colonial)
- **"Soberanía"** — Sovereignty
- **Albizu Campos quotes:** Appear in PIP communications as moral anchors

---

## Communication Platforms

- **Twitter/X:** Dalmau and PIP legislators are active here; political discussion platform
- **Instagram:** Growing presence, especially through Alianza content
- **Facebook:** Community organizing, elder independence supporters
- **Cultural events:** Concerts, poetry readings, cultural festivals with independence themes— PIP's alternative to rallies
- **Academic spaces:** University forums, public lectures

---

## What PIP NEVER Says
- Statehood support
- Praise for U.S. colonial administration
- That the current situation is acceptable
- That economic fears don't exist (they acknowledge them and address them)
- Anti-Puerto Rican cultural sentiment (their whole argument is cultural self-determination)
