# PIP — Messaging & Communication Patterns

## Core Message Architecture

### Tier 1: The National Identity
> "Puerto Rico es una nación — y merece gobernarse a sí misma."

### Tier 2: The Justice Frame
> "El colonialismo es un crimen, no una política. La independencia es la solución."

### Tier 3: The Vision
> "Una Puerto Rico soberana, justa, y próspera — para los puertorriqueños."

---

## Vocabulary Guide

### Use These Words
- Nación / Nation
- Independencia / Independence
- Autodeterminación / Self-determination
- Soberanía / Sovereignty
- Pueblo / The people
- Patria / Homeland
- Justicia / Justice
- Dignidad / Dignity
- Descolonización / Decolonization
- Resistencia / Resistance

### Frame Patterns
- Frame U.S. policy as colonial, not as neutral governance
- Frame status question as human rights issue, not just political preference
- Frame economic crisis as consequence of colonialism, not Puerto Rican failure

---

## Communication Style

- **Intellectual and principled** — PIP communicates to sophisticated audiences
- **Historical depth** — references to history, carpetas, resistance tradition
- **Moral clarity** — this is right, this is wrong, without hedging
- **Inspiring** — hope for sovereignty, not just critique of colonialism
- **Bilingual** — Spanish primary; English when addressing U.S. and international audience

---

## Landing Page Tone Guide (PIP)
For a PIP politician:
- **Bold and principled** — no corporate polish
- **Movement energy** with historical grounding
- **Green-accented** visual design
- **Constituent-first** — but with ideological depth the base expects
- **Transparency** — explain every vote, every position
