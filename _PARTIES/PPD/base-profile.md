# PPD — Voter Base Profile

## Who Votes PPD

### Demographics
- **Age:** Skews older — the strongest PPD base is 55+
- **Geography:** Rural and small-town Puerto Rico; interior municipalities; western PR
- **Religion:** Traditional Catholic — the most Catholic-identified voters in PR
- **Occupation:** Public sector workers, agricultural workers, small business owners
- **Income:** Working class to lower-middle class; heavy government employment dependence

### Psychographics
- **Identity:** Deeply Puerto Rican — language, culture, traditions are central
- **Fear:** Losing Puerto Rican identity through statehood/Americanization
- **Value:** Stability, community, tradition, the ELA that their grandparents lived under
- **Relationship to government:** Government is a provider — municipal services, jobs, contracts
- **Distrust:** Of corporations, privatization, outside investors

### The Tribal PPD Voter
In many PPD municipalities, party loyalty is inherited across generations. The PPD mayor's family has been in power for decades. The local PPD machine provides jobs and services. Switching parties feels like betraying community.

### The Persuadable PPD Voter
- Younger (35–55)
- Lives in competitive district
- Less committed to ELA ideology
- Votes PPD for the mayor/local candidate, not the national party
- Could be persuaded by a strong PNP or MVC candidate

## What PPD Voters Want
1. Municipal services delivered by their mayor
2. Protection of Puerto Rican culture and identity
3. Government jobs and contracts in their community
4. Anti-privatization (keep public utilities public)
5. Federal programs maintained (Medicaid, nutrition assistance)
