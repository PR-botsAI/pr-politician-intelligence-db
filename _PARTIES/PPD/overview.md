## Founding & Historical Significance

The PPD is the party of Luis Muñoz Marín, the most consequential political figure in Puerto Rico's modern history. Founded in 1938 during a period of massive poverty and sugar plantation domination, the PPD built its identity on land reform, industrialization, and the establishment of the Commonwealth (ELA) in 1952.

Muñoz Marín served as the first elected Governor of Puerto Rico (1948–1964) and architect of "Operation Bootstrap" (Operación Manos a la Obra) — the industrialization program that transformed PR from a rural agrarian economy into an industrial one using federal tax exemptions to attract U.S. manufacturers.

The PPD dominated Puerto Rico politics from 1940–1968 uninterruptedly — a 28-year period of transformative governance. The party created the modern Puerto Rico: the Constitution, the Commonwealth status, the industrialization, the university system expansion, and the social infrastructure.
