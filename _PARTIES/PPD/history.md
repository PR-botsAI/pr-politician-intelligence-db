# PPD — Party History 1938–2026

## Founding and the Muñoz Marín Revolution (1938–1968)

The Partido Popular Democrático was founded on July 22, 1938 by Luis Muñoz Marín, son of Luis Muñoz Rivera (the great autonomist leader), in the context of crushing poverty, sugar plantation feudalism, and colonial neglect. Puerto Rico in the 1930s had 60%+ poverty, malnutrition, sugar companies owning most of the arable land, and minimal public infrastructure.

Muñoz Marín's founding slogan — "Pan, Tierra y Libertad" (Bread, Land and Liberty) — captured the social democratic vision. The party swept to power in 1940, shattering the sugar oligarchy's political control.

### Key Muñoz Achievements
- **Land Reform:** Enforced the 500-acre limit on corporate land holdings, breaking up sugar monopolies and distributing land
- **Operation Bootstrap (1947+):** Industrialization program using federal tax exemptions (Section 936) to attract U.S. manufacturers — transformed PR from agrarian to industrial in one generation
- **Commonwealth Constitution (1952):** Created the Estado Libre Asociado; Puerto Rico became a self-governing territory with its own constitution
- **University Expansion:** UPR became a major research institution
- **Infrastructure:** Roads, electrification, water systems, hospitals built across the island
- **Education:** Universal primary education achieved

Muñoz Marín served as governor from 1949 to 1965 — four terms. He chose not to run in 1964, anointing Roberto Sánchez Vilella as successor. The PPD held power uninterruptedly for 28 years (1940–1968).

## First Loss and Alternating Power (1968–2004)

In 1968, the PPD lost to PNP's Luis Ferré. This began the alternating pattern that characterized PR politics for the next 48 years:
- 1968: PNP (Ferré)
- 1972: PPD (Hernández Colón)
- 1976: PNP (Romero Barceló)
- 1980: PNP (Romero Barceló)
- 1984: PPD (Hernández Colón)
- 1988: PPD (Hernández Colón)
- 1992: PNP (Rosselló)
- 1996: PNP (Rosselló)
- 2000: PPD (Calderón)
- 2004: PPD (Acevedo Vilá)

### Rafael Hernández Colón (1973–1977, 1985–1993)
Two-term governor, the face of PPD's ELA enhancement strategy. Proposed the Compact of Free Association concept. Fought hard against Section 936 elimination. His second administration (1985–1993) was marked by economic growth and the PPD's most serious push for an enhanced ELA.

## The Fiscal Crisis and PPD Decline (2005–2024)

### Acevedo Vilá (2005–2009)
Faced federal corruption charges but was acquitted. His administration coincided with the start of PR's fiscal crisis. Section 936 elimination in 1996 began showing its effects: manufacturing declined, unemployment rose, debt increased.

### García Padilla (2013–2017)
The most consequential recent PPD governor for all the wrong reasons. In 2015, he declared Puerto Rico's $72 billion debt "unpayable" — a politically honest but economically devastating statement. The U.S. Congress responded by passing PROMESA in 2016, creating the Fiscal Oversight and Management Board. The PPD lost the 2016 election badly.

### The Three Losses (2016, 2020, 2024)
- **2016:** Lost governor to PNP under Ricardo Rosselló
- **2020:** Lost governor to Pedro Pierluisi (PNP) despite Rosselló's scandal — PPD could not capitalize
- **2024:** Lost governor to Jenniffer González Colón; PNP won supermajority

**The PPD's municipal strength is now its only island-wide institutional base.** 41 mayors in 2024 is actually an increase from some prior cycles. The municipal machine is more durable than the party's electoral appeal for governor.

## The Resident Commissioner Win (2024)

Pablo José Hernández Rivera's victory as Resident Commissioner (beating former Senator William Villafañe by ~105,000 votes) was PPD's major 2024 win and provides a platform in Washington. This matters for federal legislation affecting PR, including status bills and federal program funding.
