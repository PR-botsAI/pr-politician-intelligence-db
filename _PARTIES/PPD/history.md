# PPD — Party History

## Founding & The Muñoz Marín Era (1938–1968)

**Luis Muñoz Marín** founded the PPD in 1938 during Puerto Rico's most desperate era — 90% poverty, sugar monoculture, U.S. corporate domination, no elected governor.

His coalition: jíbaros (rural farmers), workers, intellectuals who wanted economic transformation without independence's economic risks.

**"Pan, Tierra y Libertad"** — the founding promise.

### Operation Bootstrap (Operación Manos a la Obra) — 1948
The defining economic achievement of PPD governance:
- Used federal tax exemptions (Section 936) to attract U.S. manufacturers
- Transformed PR from sugar plantation economy to industrial manufacturing
- Created the pharmaceutical industry that still dominates PR economy
- Built University of Puerto Rico network
- Built public housing, roads, electricity infrastructure
- **Controversies:** Forced sterilization program (La Operación); destroyed some rural communities; created dependency

### The Commonwealth (1952)
- PPD drafted PR's Constitution
- Established Estado Libre Asociado (ELA) — Commonwealth
- Ended U.S. military governorship era
- Created elected Governor, Legislature, Supreme Court
- **Debate:** Was this the best PR could get in 1952? Or a missed opportunity for independence? PPD says the former; PIP/MVC say the latter.

## Decline & Competition (1968–present)

### 1968: First Loss
- Muñoz Marín chose not to run; his successor lost to Luis Ferré (PNP)
- Ended 28 years of PPD dominance
- Marked beginning of competitive two-party era

### The Back-and-Forth Era (1968–2012)
- PPD and PNP alternated power roughly every 4–8 years
- Rafael Hernández Colón: 2 terms (1973–1977, 1985–1993)
- Sila María Calderón: First female governor (2001–2005)
- Aníbal Acevedo Vilá: Prosecuted but acquitted of corruption (2005–2009)
- Alejandro García Padilla: Last PPD governor (2013–2017)

### The Debt Crisis (2014–2017)
- García Padilla declared PR's $72 billion debt "unpayable" (2015)
- PROMESA passed by Congress, imposing Fiscal Oversight Board (2016)
- PPD blamed for the debt by PNP; PPD blames Congress and Section 936 elimination
- **The defining failure of recent PPD governance** — they lost the economic credibility argument

### Post-2017: The Wilderness
- Lost 2016 to Rosselló (PNP)
- Lost 2020 to Pierluisi (PNP) — even amid PNP's Telegramgate chaos
- Lost 2024 to González Colón (PNP)
- 3 consecutive governor losses is historically unprecedented for PPD

### Why Is PPD Losing?
1. **The debt crisis legacy** — being blamed for economic collapse
2. **Demographic change** — PPD's rural base is shrinking as population moves to cities or leaves PR
3. **MVC/PIP siphoning left vote** — progressive PPDers have moved to MVC
4. **PD siphoning religious conservatives** — some PPD social conservatives moved to Proyecto Dignidad
5. **The statehood momentum** — multiple plebiscites showing majority statehood support
6. **No charismatic leader** — no one of Muñoz Marín's stature has emerged
