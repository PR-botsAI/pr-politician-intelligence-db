# PPD — Internal Party Structure

## Current Power Configuration (Post-2024)

The PPD faces a structural challenge: they control 41 of 78 municipalities (mayors) but are in the minority in both legislative chambers and the governorship. This creates a divided party with two centers of gravity.

### Municipal Power Base (The Real PPD Power)
- 41 mayors = the spine of PPD organizational structure
- Mayors control municipal hiring, contracts, services
- PPD mayors in large municipalities: significant local power bases
- Municipal machine survives without Fortaleza (governor's mansion)
- Example: If Arecibo mayor is PPD while Robles (PNP) is the representative, they compete for constituent credit

## Internal Factions (2024–2026)

### The "ELA Soberano" Faction
Wants to enhance the Commonwealth relationship — more autonomy, more sovereignty within U.S. framework. Argues the current ELA is inadequate and the PPD must push for a genuine enhanced Commonwealth. This wing is ideologically clear but faces a U.S. Congress that has no interest in creating this option.

### The Status-Agnostic / Governance Faction
"Forget status — let's talk about what actually affects people's lives." This faction wants PPD to de-emphasize status and compete on governance: anti-corruption, public services, economic development. The 2024 Resident Commissioner winner (Hernández Rivera) represents this pragmatic wing.

### The Old Guard / Municipal Machine
Long-serving mayors and their networks. Deep roots in patronage systems. Less interested in ideological debates, more focused on maintaining local power. The municipal machine is PPD's most reliable institutional asset.

### The Progressive-Adjacent Wing
PPD members who feel left behind by MVC's rise. Some sympathy for progressive positions but unwilling to leave the PPD machine. Internal pressure to move left on social issues.

## How PPD Selects Candidates

- Same primary system as PNP
- Municipal primaries are often the most competitive within PPD (mayoral races)
- PPD primaries for governor have been contentious: party has not resolved succession question clearly
- At the legislative level, PPD primaries are typically quieter given smaller caucus

---

## Relationship with Democratic Party

- PPD formally aligns with Democratic Party
- Resident Commissioner Hernández Rivera will caucus with Democrats
- But PPD in PR is not identical to mainland Democrats: PPD has social conservatives, rural Catholics who wouldn't fit in a typical Democratic coalition
- The alliance is primarily strategic (federal legislative access) not ideological
- Democratic Party is split on Puerto Rican statehood — some Democrats support it (Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, who is Puerto Rican), some don't prioritize it

## PPD's Structural Advantage: Mayors

The 41 PPD mayors represent a survival mechanism the party relies on:
- Even after three consecutive governor losses (2016 partial, 2020, 2024)
- Even after losing the legislature
- PPD mayors keep the machine alive, keep staff employed, keep the network connected
- This is why PPD can bounce back — their municipal base is durable
- **Implication for us:** PPD mayors are excellent landing page clients. They have budgets, they have reelection needs, and they're operating somewhat independently from the weakened party center.
