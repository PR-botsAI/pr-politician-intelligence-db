# PPD — Internal Structure & Party Mechanics

## Leadership (Post-2024)

### Party President
- **Jesús Manuel Ortiz González** — PPD President
- Leading the party through its worst legislative period in decades
- Challenge: rebuilding after 3 consecutive governor losses

### Resident Commissioner
- **Pablo José Hernández Rivera** — notable PPD WIN in 2024
- Non-voting U.S. House representative
- Most significant PPD victory in the 2024 cycle

### Senate Minority
- PPD holds minority seats in Senate (Thomas Rivera Schatz (PNP) is President)
- PPD senators serve as opposition voice

### House Minority
- PPD holds minority seats in Cámara
- Opposition role on legislation

## The Municipal Machine — PPD's True Power

With 41 mayors (2024), PPD controls:
- Municipal budgets totaling hundreds of millions
- Municipal government employment (thousands of jobs per municipality)
- Contracts for local services, construction, maintenance
- The day-to-day face of government for rural Puerto Ricans

**This is why PPD survives despite legislative losses.** Mayors are more popular than the party. When a PPD mayor fixes your roads, gets you a job, or helps your family with a government service — you're loyal to that mayor.

## Internal Factions

### The Old Guard
- Traditional populares rooted in Muñoz Marín's ELA tradition
- Cultural nationalists, rural base
- Favor maintaining status quo ELA

### The Reformers
- Younger PPD politicians who want to modernize the party
- Less committed to ELA as identity, more focused on governance
- Some are open to status plebiscite or new formula

### The Municipal Barons
- Powerful mayors who run their municipalities as personal fiefdoms
- Sometimes more powerful than legislative leadership
- Key coalition builders for presidential selection
