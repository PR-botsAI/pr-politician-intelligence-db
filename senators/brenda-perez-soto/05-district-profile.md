## The Geographic Challenge

Brenda Pérez Soto represents the most geographically diverse senatorial district in terms of terrain:
- **Coastal corridor:** Manatí → Arecibo → Hatillo → Camuy → Quebradillas (north-south: PR-2 axis)
- **Mountain interior:** Lares and Utuado accessible only via mountain highways PR-10, PR-111, PR-129
- **Travel time:** Driving from Quebradillas to Utuado takes 1+ hours through mountain roads
- **This is why digital reach matters so much for her specifically:** She physically cannot be in all municipalities regularly

**Our key pitch to Brenda:**
> "Senadora, su distrito va desde las playas de Quebradillas hasta las montañas de Utuado. Una plataforma digital le permite estar presente en todos sus municipios las 24 horas del día — sin moverse de su oficina."

## Top Constituent Issues by Municipality

### Arecibo (Hub)
- PRASA Northern Region instability (same crisis as Robles is addressing)
- Flooding (Río Grande de Arecibo)
- Economic development
- Hospital/healthcare access for the region

### Hatillo
- Dairy industry support and agricultural policy
- Rural road access
- Festival de Máscaras support (cultural/tourism)

### Camuy
- Camuy Caves development (Parque de las Cavernas) — major tourism potential
- Rural services
- Coastal infrastructure

### Quebradillas
- Fishing community support
- Small-town services
- Coastal erosion and infrastructure

### Lares
- Hurricane Maria recovery (mountain municipalities had worst damage)
- Coffee agriculture revival
- Road access (PR-111, PR-129 mountain roads)
- Historical significance management (Grito de Lares)

### Utuado
- Dos Bocas reservoir (PRASA water source for Norte region)
- Hurricane Maria recovery — still ongoing
- Mountain road access
- Economic poverty: among PR's poorest municipalities

### Manatí
- Pharmaceutical manufacturing base
- Eastern coastal infrastructure
- Flooding issues

## Political Landscape Within the District

- **Coastal corridor:** Competitive PNP/PPD; Arecibo and Hatillo swing between parties
- **Mountain interior:** Historically PPD (Lares in particular has strong PPD/independence tradition)
- **Manatí:** Competitive, leans PNP in recent cycles
- **2024:** Pérez Soto won as part of PNP island-wide wave — not necessarily safe in a non-wave year
- **2028 vulnerability:** If mountain interior municipalities (Lares, Utuado) swing back to PPD, her seat becomes competitive
